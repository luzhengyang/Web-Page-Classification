Miami Beach Hotels

With long sandy beaches and clear blue seas, Miami Beach is a tropical paradise, complete with palm trees and balmy weather all year round. On a strip of land, parallel to the city of Miami, the area feels like an island, complete with luxurious resorts and restaurants overlooking the sea. At night, the area hosts some of the most exclusive nightclubs in the world, becoming a playground for the rich and famous.

Things to see

Wander down Ocean Drive during the day and you’ll see beautiful people enjoying the white sandy beach, with rollerbladers and joggers sharing the sidewalk. Take a seat outside one of the upmarket pavement cafes or restaurants, and you can enjoy watching the shady palms and the calm blue ocean from your vantage point. At night, the Art Deco buildings are lit by neon, and the area becomes a buzzing social hive. The North Miami area is home to Surfside Beach, a wide, white-sanded beach which is a quieter alternative to the more central beaches. The Port of Miami is known as the cruise capital of the world, and you’ll see many of the giant cruise ships departing for voyages around the Bahamas, Caribbean, Mexico, and beyond, giving passengers on board a breathtaking view of the skyline as the ship pulls away. Lincoln Road Mall is a palm tree lined, pedestrianized street full of upscale stores, boutiques, art galleries, and sidewalk cafes, giving it a laidback vibe both day and night. If you need some time for quiet contemplation, Miami Beach Botanical Garden is an urban greenspace that’s free to enjoy. Wander around enjoying the fragrance of exotic flowers, take a yoga class in beautiful surroundings, or simply sit under a tree and take in the abundant horticulture of the area.

Hotels in Miami Beach

Luxury hotels in Miami Beach are often found along the beachfront, with many resorts offering swimming pools, health spas, steam rooms, and concierge services, so you can sit back and enjoy your vacation time. Guestrooms will offer amenities such as air conditioning, minibars, coffee makers, premium bedding, and bathrobes. Many of the Art Deco buildings of the South Beach area have been turned into boutique hotels, offering oversized bathtubs, individual interior design, room service, and free WiFi. There are also budget hotels and hostels a couple of blocks away from the beach, offering clean, comfortable rooms with cable television and free toiletries.

Where to stay

The South Beach neighborhood is famous for its lively nightlife, stretches of sandy beach, and diverse population. During the day you’ll see vacationing families enjoying the beach and the eclectic attractions along the seafront, and at night the area becomes all about fun, with clubs and restaurants lit up along the coast. The Miami Beach area has a distinctive, modern skyline, with glimmering skyscrapers, and high-rise buildings everywhere you look. However, as you get closer, you’ll notice a lot of Art Deco influences wherever you go, and this gives many of the streets a quirky, unique feel.

How to get to Miami Beach

Those visiting as part of a cruise itinerary will arrive via the Port of Miami, an impressive facility capable of holding even the largest cruise liners, and welcoming all the biggest name operators. There are convenient taxi links between the airport and the cruise ships, and some tour operators will book your shuttle as part of the package. Haulover Park Marina is an ideal spot for independent sailors to dock, as it’s conveniently located in the north end of the city. Miami, FL (MPB-Public Seaplane Base) can be found between the city of Miami and the Miami beach area, and as well as being open for public use, you can book rides to see the area from a new perspective.
