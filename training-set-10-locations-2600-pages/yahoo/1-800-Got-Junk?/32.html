CASABUBBLE



We all want to relive our childhood dreams… And sleeping under the stars is one of them.  But we are not all ready to put up with the discomfort that implies (the cold, the damp, the mosquitoes). So we have developed astonishing transparent structures to combine dream and comfort.   An eco-design by French designer Stéphane Dumas and Frederic Richard for development, these “dream bubbles” are the ¬first ecotourism living spaces that can be self-sufficient in energy and use bio-air-conditioning.
This new approach is based on the principles of: Minimum energy, Minimum material, Maximum comfort for a Maximum interaction with the environment, and easy storage.  The idea goes well beyond a “night under the stars”.  By incorporating the wilds in an economic and educational dynamic, the designer seeks to show how legitimate nature reserves are and to open up new possibilities for up-market green tourism.  The impact on the environment is extremely low and plants and animals live undisturbed in their natural habitat.

THE HUMBER COLLEGE EXHIBIT


This one-of-a-kind exhibit will take visitors on an inspiring journey through the challenges of building green and will be presented in a market like setting featuring interactive multimedia environment aimed at a younger demographic and social relevance.

The intent is to present active and passive green design strategies in an environment focused on sharing of knowledge and information with the consumer. Within this consumerist environment we plan to break down boundaries and make direct links between the design, education, and manufacturing sectors of the industry. The exhibits will educate consumers on green home design and technology, as well as connecting them to experts so they can get started.
