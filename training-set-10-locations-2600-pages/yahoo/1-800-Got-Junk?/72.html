    • SteveN answered 5 years ago
    The city of Montreal has a few "EcoCentres" located around the city that you can take old appliances to so you can dispose of them. You are entitle to up to fifteen trips per year at no cost to drop things off there, as long as the size does not exceed a certain amount (measured in cubic metres).

    The problem is that I did not see any mention that the city of Montreal will come and pick these up for you. I think you have to get them there yourself.

    What you should do is check with your local burrough, or call the City of Montreal, if you have absolutely no way to get the washer and dryer to the depot.

    I know that out where I live, there is a guy in a truck we call "Jean Ramasse". He comes around every garbage day looking for old furniture, appliances, and scrap metal. Pops that all in his old Ford pickup truck and drives on down the street. May want to see if you can catch one of these guys.

    And finally, I doubt you will get any money for the washer/dryer, but if one or both are still working, you might be able to convince the Salvation Army or Sun Youth to come pick them up. They can give them to someone less fortunate who needs a replacement.
