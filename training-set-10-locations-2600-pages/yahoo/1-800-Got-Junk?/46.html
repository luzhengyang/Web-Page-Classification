Speakers

Brian Scudamore

How many business people do you know who got rich from garbage? Brian Scudamore quite literally turned other people’s trash into treasure through the creation of 1-800-GOT-JUNK? His story of starting out with just one truck and eventually built a multinational company will resonate with OTA carriers who sprouted from similar owner-operator roots as well as inspire those who continue to aspire for growth. Creating a worldwide brand, says Scudamore, comes down to articulating your vision and building a culture around the right people. Scudamore is an entrepreneur recognized for his passion, vision, and faith in people. Punchy and vividly anecdotal, Scudamore shows you how easy it is for any company to learn new tricks, improve each day and attract a ton of positive media attention, sharing his strategies and bottom line results. Scudamore has appeared on Oprah, Dr. Drew’s Clutter Cleaners, Dr. Phil, CNN, ABC Nightline, Good Morning America, The Today Show, and The View. His impressive story has been told in Fortune Magazine, Business Week, The New York Times, Huffington Post, and The Wall Street Journal, to name a few. 1-800-GOT-JUNK? is currently the starring junk removal attraction on the hit A&E reality show, Hoarders.

Thomas Frey

Unmanned vehicles, airborne delivery drones, commercial 3-D printers, ‘hyperloop’ transport tubes are all realities. How will they one day affect trucking and logistics, if at all? Futurist Speaker Thomas Frey will shed light on that very question.  Goggle’s #1 rated futurist, Frey joins the OTA convention  to share his revolutionary thinking about the future and new technologies and what  it means for your  business, the global economy and geopolitics. Author of the 2011 book “Communicating with the Future, Frey is the Executive Director at the DaVinci Institute in Boulder, Colo., where he and his team develop original research studies, which enables him to speak on unusual topics, translating trends into unique opportunities.

His keynote talks and multimedia presentations have captivated people in high levels of government and  Fortune 500 companies including NASA, IBM, AT&T, Hewlett-Packard, Boeing, Capital One, Bell Canada, Visa, and Ford Motor Company,  among others. Because of his work inspiring inventors and other innovative thinkers, The Denver Post and Seattle Post Intelligencer have referred to him as the “Dean of Futurists”. Before launching the DaVinci Institute, Frey spent 15 years at IBM as an engineer and designer where he received over 270 awards.  He currently writes a weekly “Future Trend Report” newsletter and a weekly column for FuturistSpeaker.com.

Teresa Cascioli

One of Canada’s top business minds, Teresa Cascioli’s tenacious tactics saw her lead one of the country’s most successful income trust public offerings. As CEO of Lakeport Brewing, she took the company from bankruptcy to a major player and eventually into a $201-million sale to Labatt. Her slogan and innovative pricing model “24 for $24” changed the way beer was marketed. As a result, Lakeport brands catapulted onto the best seller list while big brewers scrambled to enter the discount beer market. Cascioli is now the chief creative director for her own marketing firm, Tess Creative. Previously, she served as strategic advisor to Labatt and KPMG. She has been featured on Profit and Chatelaine Magazine’s lists of Canada’s most successful women entrepreneurs, and was named Entrepreneur of the Year by Canada’s Venture Capital and Private Equity Association as well as by Ernst & Young in their Turnaround Category. In 2012, Cascioli was awarded the Queen’s Diamond Jubilee Medal. In keynotes, she teaches companies and entrepreneurs how to make it big – drawing on her finely honed expertise in turnaround, growth, and change management.

Jeff "Odie" Espenship

A former USAF fighter pilot, instructor and current international airline pilot, Jeff “Odie” Espenship  understands how to operate and lead a team in a high-risk and often-dangerous work environment.  Now also a popular motivational speaker and founder of Target Leadership, Jeff uses his unique experiences to connect with audiences and explain how to work safely and build a safe and secure company culture.  After receiving his Bachelor’s degree in Political Science, Jeff spent a year cheating certain death and destruction earning his USAFpilot wings. Due to his outgoing personality and upbeat disposition, his squadron mates quickly gave him his moniker –  “Odie” aptly named after the popular cartoon Garfield’s sidekick. But a tragic incident would shape the path for the rest of his life. An accident took the life of Jeff’s brother, as well as the life of another pilot. This significant event ignited the spark that helped create what Target Leadership is today. “They inspire me to be a better person every day. They also serve as a reminder for me to be sure my words are congruent with my deeds at all levels of family, business, and friends,” he says.

Gerry Dee

Who says growing up in Scarborough isn’t comedy gold?  Gerry Dee began his comedy career in a very different fashion. Today he’s the star of the hit sitcom Mr. D, but Gerry first spent 10 years as a high school teacher before making the switch to full time comedy and acting in 2003. The son of Scottish immigrant parents, Gerry was born in Scarborough, Ont. and grew up in Toronto. He currently splits his time between living in Los Angeles and Toronto. In 1999, he won Toronto’s Funniest New Comic competition. He would later tape his own half hour Comedy Now Special on CTV and The Comedy Network in 2001. A year later he was the first Canadian in 27 years to win the prestigious San Francisco International Comedy Competition. In 2008 Gerry won Best Comic in Canada at the 2008 Canadian Comedy Awards. His new sitcom Mr. D, which he created, writes and stars in is based on Gerry  real-life experiences as a high school teacher. The premise shines the light on teaching from the teacher’s point of view, and as it turns out, teachers don’t know everything, especially Mr. D – who struggles to keep one step ahead of his students.  You can also watch Gerry weekly on The Score with his very humorous segment called Gerry Dee-Sports Reporter. Gerry is a former Varsity hockey player and golfer. He is married with two children.

Derek Thompson

Derek Thompson takes a close look at Millennials — the most educated yet most under-employed generation in modern history. What does this demographic like to buy, wear, and drive? Why are they purchasing fewer houses and cars, rejecting the status symbols of previous generations, and how does this affect the economy? Thompson, a senior editor at The Atlantic, reveals why they think and act the way they do.

Derek Thompson is the senior editor for business and economic coverage for The Atlantic. He oversees The Atlantic business section online and writes the business column for the magazine. “[Thompson] writes and edits economic news that is readable, informative, and often entertaining,” said the Huffington Post.

Thompson is also an adjunct professor at the Columbia Journalism School, where he teaches digital journalism. He has appeared on numerous lists, including Folio’s 15 Under 30 in Media. He appears frequently on television and radio, particularly CNBC and NPR.
