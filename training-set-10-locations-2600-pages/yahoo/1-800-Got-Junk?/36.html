    Leon's at the Roundhouse is a very unique and interesting shopping experience.  They have re-appropriated an old locomotive roundhouse as a furniture display room...very cool and unique use of the space while still feeling "authentic" in terms of the history.

    I like much of their furniture here.  I was primarily shopping for couches.  As for couches, I find that they actually have very reasonable prices, perhaps almost on par with Ikea (or slightly more expensive) but for much higher quality.  I found a nice modern couch here that I was very satisfied with.  They delivered it for free too (although minus half a star for the delivery people coming in the afternoon when they were scheduled in the morning).  

    My saleswoman, Ellen, rocked.  She was friendly and knowledgeable while not being pushy at all.  She even remembered my name on my second visit to the store to actually purchase the couch.  I felt very comfortable directing commission dollars her way.

    Now I could see how one would have a negative experience not shopping with Ellen there.  On my second visit, I was stalked by several salemen at about a 10 foot distance.   I felt like I was in Jurassic Park  being hunted by a pack of velociraptors.  The one who approached could only be described as "creepy" and aggressive.  Yuck.  Thankfully, I spied Ellen who rescued me from the creepy velociraptor man.

    Ok, and here's what really earned my repeat business.  They have a very well-advertised 60 day price protection.  Well, I looked online a few weeks after purchasing my couch and found the price was $100 cheaper.  I thought, surely they'll have a fine print excuse to get out of this, but nope.  I walked in, found Ellen, and she was legitimately cheerful that she could save me $125 in total with the price change.  Her manager had to process it which took a solid 25 minutes...you would think he was launching the space shuttle as long as it took.

    Anyway, my overall impression of the Leon's here is very positive. Their selection is modern and appropriate for apartment/condo owners in terms of size.  Ellen rocked as a saleswoman, although some of their other staff were really off-putting.  And with actually honoring the price protection, they will definitely get my repeat business.

    This Leon's is located close to the Roger's Centre/Skydome and while they win some points for great prices and stocking slightly more "condo-sized" furntiure, the selection leaves a lot to be desired. I know taste is personal, but there is a lot of UGLY furniture here.

    When I decided to buy a three-seater couch, the service started off as just "meh, I've experienced better" and quickly moved into "Eek! Methinks not" territory. Of course, this wasn't until AFTER I purchased the couch.

    I was told by the customer service rep that they would remove my old couch when they brought my new one. I was very specific, I live in a condo building that does not have any place to dispose of large pieces of furniture and there is no where for them to "leave it" on the curb. They told me "no problem! we deliver to condos all the time, we understand your situation." I was actually out shopping with my parents that day and they overheard the ENTIRE situation.

    When the delivery folks actually showed up, they told me all they can do is bring the couch downstairs to leave on the curb (uh, I live on KING STREET and there is a pub at bottom of my building, you can't just leave a couch down there). I immediately got on the phone with Leon's and was told by the woman who answered that indeed, this is standard practice and they NEVER would have told me they would remove the couch. Except they did.

    I asked to speak to the manager, I was told the manager does not take phone calls customers. I asked to speak to the rep who told me that the couch could be removed, and I was told he was not able to come to the phone either. My salesperson (different from the rep) called me back and told me that this is just the way it is. I complained some more and asked to speak to the manager. Again I was told no.

    I later called and asked to speak to the manager directly and although they tried to give me the run around I told them I would keep calling back until I spoke to a manager. The manager finally came on the phone and although she apologized that I had so much trouble getting her on the phone, she backed up her staff and said "I think you misunderstood the salesperson" and she couldn't do anything for me. Totally not pleased.

    I happened to call my parents after this exchange and my Dad, quite annoyed on my behalf, went to the store to explain that he head the same thing to the manager who also was not impressed.

    After a lot of hassle on the phone with these guys, feeling like I was mislead (or even lied to), and paying $100 to 1-800-Got-Junk to remove my old sofa bed couch - the great deal at Leon's totally wasn't worth it.

    To further prove how awful Leon's is at communication, I paid an extra $50 or $60 to get the couch treated so it wouldn't stain. They never explained to me that someone would have to come to my condo AFTER purchasing the couch. And the guy from this out-sourced company said, "Yeah, most people are surprised when this happens."

    I definitely won't return to Leon's and I advise you to shop there at your own risk.

    This place gets 1 star because the actual retail space is fabulous.  However, after the magic of the overal esthetic wears off, you are left with ...ho-hum...the furniture.

    My friend was shopping for a sectional sofa...nothing extraordinary or fancy, just a simple grey sectional with right side lounger section.   Well, we spotted a few that were inexpensive with the appropriate dimensions for her space...yay!  However, our glee quickly turned to disappointment once we spoke to the salesperson and were told that the sofas we liked, actually, most if not all of the sofas, were not available in any other fabric, footing or configurations than what was displayed on the floor.  WTF??!

    How can you not give people options?!  It made no sense...up was down, black was white, wrong was right...I'm talking Bizzaro World people.

    Our salesperson explained that the reason they don't offer options is to keep the pricing down and ensure timely shipping of the furnitre, or some other BS like that...I honestly cannot say for certain what she said because I was in shock, still trying to process the fact that we couldn't actually chose a fabric or configuration to suit my friend's sectional sofa needs.

    On the plus side, they do have a lot of TVs and some kick ass refridgerators and stoves...but even that selection is limited.  However all of this was negated by another simple fact...wait for it...

    THEY DON'T HAVE CUSTOMER PARKING!!! GASP!

    Overall impression? Meh!
