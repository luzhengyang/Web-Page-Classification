Wishbone

Posted by WLCO in Membership on 3/21/2013

Chef Joel Nickson founded Wishbone Restaurant at 1800 W. Grand Ave in July 1990 with 23 seats, nine stools, and a half dozen paintings by his mother, Lia Nickson.

The restaurant was a family affair with many members of his family helping build out the space with their own hands.

As one of the first chef-owned independent restaurants in the Wicker Park area, Wishbone boasted large portions of amazingly fresh food and quickly became a popular hangout for artists and local businesses of the area.

A second Wishbone was opened in 1992 with 170 seats at 1001 W. Washington--at a time when Chicago's West Loop seemed an uncharted territory. Wishbone was an early participant in the emergence of the neighborhood from a commercial area to one of the city's culinary hot spots. Today, beneath lights designed by Joel’s brother Greg, diners can start their day with Corn Pancakes or Shrimp & Grits and dig into dinners such as Blackened Catfish or North Carolina Pulled pork, often served by Joel’s own children.
