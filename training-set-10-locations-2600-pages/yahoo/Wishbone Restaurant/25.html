Wishbone Restaurant – A Disgrace to Southern Cooking

Harrison | November 3, 2013 | West Loop | No Comments

Wishbone Restaurant is a southern and soul food restaurant located in the West Loop and Roscoe Village that has a reputation for being “really good southern food”. Really? What a fucking joke. This place is a disgrace to its cuisine. The menu only had one appetizer – shrimp and grits, which were incredible. It was very garlicky, well-seasoned and the cook on the grits were phenomenal. But that was the only positive I found to their food.

Hoppin John is a wildly popular dish down south, and Wishbone had something the “call” hoppin john on their menu. What they served me was a fabricated combination of separate ingredients. Everything in the hoppin john seemed to be made separately. It starts with a base of rice, which was cooked by itself. It was then topped with black eyed peas, which tasted like it came from a can and microwaved to heat it up a little bit. It was then topped with green onion and what seemed to be pre-shredded cheese. Are you fucking serious? You won’t even melt the fucking cheese? Not only that, but who the fuck uses pre-shredded cheese? This ain’t no Wal-mart, this is the Chicago food scene. After smothering it with hot sauce, it almost tasted like something.

Then there was the mac n cheese. This mac n cheese looked like it was pre-made several hours earlier and set on the counter all day until it was thrown in the microwave and served. The cheese curdled up and created these rock-hard chunks mixed in with cold noodles. Fucking sick.

And the coleslaw? Ok, so cabbage needs to be rubbed with salt and marinated in a vinaigrette for a few hours to achieve tenderness for vinegar-based coleslaw. This coleslaw seemed as if it was just shredded cabbage mixed with some vinegar and pepper, then heated in the microwave, which rendered it stuff and tasteless. What’s funny is that this was the warmest food they served me.

The burger… oh god, the fucking burger. First, they claim to have this Meyer grass-fed beef. This beef definitely was not grass-fed. There is a huge difference in taste between corn-fed and grass-fed beef, and this place is full of shit when it comes to their beef. It’s low-grade corn-fed ground beef. I ordered my burger medium-rare. What I got was a completely dry, over-charred hockey puck. They topped it with a slice of cheese that didn’t even begin to melt. At least if you’re going to puck my burger, have the common decency to melt the damn cheese over it. No fucking excuse! I didn’t quite understand at first why they put ketchup and mustard by me when they brought out the burger, but now I know.

To the kitchen staff at Wishbone – stop taking shortcuts. You are a disgrace to a cuisine that’s normally made from hours of preparation and a highly involved cooking process. You should all go and start up another shitty fake Irish bar, because that’s about the quality of food you’re serving people.
