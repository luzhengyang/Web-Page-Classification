Joel Nickson Chef/Owner
Born in Roswell, NM, Joel grew up in North Carolina and northern New Jersey, where, at 16 running away from home, he first encountered kitchen work at a soul food restaurant in Harlem called Pauline's Passion Pit. From there, he received a very different education from the renowned Culinary Institute of America (where Tony Bourdain was a classmate) and a series of kitchen posts taking him from Ernie's (featured in Vertigo) in San Francisco to La Tulipe in Greenwich Village and La Quinta in Palm Desert, among others in Phoenix, Denver, Chapel Hill, and Miami. Prompted by his brothers to open his own restaurant in Chicago, Joel landed in the Windy City from Wilmington, NC and within eighteen months (and considerable sweat equity) the first Wishbone opened at 1800 W. Grand Avenue in the summer of 1990. Joel moved recently from Humboldt Park to Oak Park with his wife, Julie, and their two sons, Tyler and Clay, and daughter, Delia in anticipation of another Wishbone opening in Berwyn in late February 2007.
