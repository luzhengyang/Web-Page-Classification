About Parking Near Union Oyster House

Taste the finest New England fare that Boston has to offer at the Union Oyster House. The Union Oyster House is the longest-running restaurant in the United States, and was deemed a National Historic Landmark in May 2003. The restaurant serves traditional New England specialties such as oysters, clams, and lobsters, as well as poultry, baked beans, steak, and chops. Many notable patrons have dined at the famed restaurant, including the Kennedy family and Daniel Webster. It is also rumored that when he was exiled, the former King of France Louis Philippe had at one point lived upstairs from the restaurant.
