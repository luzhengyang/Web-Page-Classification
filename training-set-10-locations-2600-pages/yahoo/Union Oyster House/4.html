        About Union Oyster House:
        Only in a city that is steeped in history could you find a seafood Restaurant that is on the National Register of Historic Places. The Union Oyster House has been in continual operation since 1826, making it the oldest continually operating Restaurant in the country. Guests at this Boston Restaurant can sidle up to the same Oyster Bar that has welcomed hungry guests for nearly 200 years.

        Watch as the shuckers do their thing with many different types of oysters, and they can also explain the subtle differences between the various types of oysters. This historic Boston Restaurant offers much more than freshly shucked oysters - although, for some, that is more than enough. Also on the menu is savory fare such as Lobster Ravioli, Broiled Fresh Boston Scrod, Broiled Sea Scallops and a Broiled Seafood Platter. A selection of steaks, chops and chicken is available for those who do not like seafood.

        If you manage to save room dessert then consider the Warm Apple Cobbler, Chocolate Lava Cake, or Boston Cream Pie. A selection of delicious international coffees can also help create a perfect ending to your meal at Union Oyster House.
