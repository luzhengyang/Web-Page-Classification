City Highlight: Boston Tourism

August 1, 2014Shannon

The thing I love most about Boston is all the history we have to offer here. No matter what your niche, there’s something to tickle your fancy for at least one full day. So, here’s a locale’s skinny on the Top 10 Boston Sites to see (and then what I think this list should be).

  1. Boston Commons/Public Garden; Definitely the tourist thing to do while you’re here. Swan Boats (which I recently realized I’ve never done myself), the Ducklings Statue (been ever Bostonian Christmas card at least once), and Frog Pond (wading pool in the summer, ice skating rink in the winter!).
  2. Fenway Park; Hell yes. Avoid driving in- take the over-crowded T (subway) or walk if you can. Parking is cheapest at about $40 (more than the tickets). But definitely worth a go while you’re here. My boyfriend’s favourite seats of all times were on the Green Monstah! (Mine were 3rd row behind home plate, but I won’t brag).
  3. Freedom Trail; a 2.5 mile trail covering 16 sites of the Revolutionary War. If you come in early spring, or mid-fall… great, have fun. But the majority of the year (40% winter, 40% blistering summer), you don’t want to walk a million miles around the city half-ass seeing city-sites you could’ve just taken a Duck Tour to see.
  4. Boston Duck Tour; Duck Tours are fantastic and usually pretty fun. Even I learned things about Boston when I brought my out-of-state cousins on one last summer. Another good tour (a little different) is the Ghost and Graveyards tour. You take a trolley around the city and tour all the haunted and mysterious things that have happened in Boston!
  5. Bunker Hill Monument; de-bunked. Climb up a steep hill and there’s a really tall stone monument. You can see it from any high-point in the city, so take a look from some place more interesting and save yourself the 30 min trek up the red line.
  6. Legal Seafoods; Pass. Union Oyster House is better. Less chain-y.
  7. Fanueil Hall Marketplace/Quincy Market; Definetly a go-see. Quincy Market is made up of three buildings. The outer two are filled with shops and a few restaurants (ranging from typical Coach and Harley Davidson to Condom World and Funusual!) The center building is Fanueil Hall- filled with every ethnicity of food you could think of, including some great bars on top and street performers surrounding it to entertain for your change! Definitely head there for lunch, ice cream, or coffee.
  8. New England Aquarium (specifically: IMAX); I’ll admit, this is one of my favourite places. But I’m not sure that if you only had two days in Boston, I would suggest you go there. The Aquarium is cool; big circular tank in the middle, seals out front, sea lions of back, giant penguin exhibit on the bottom floor surrounding the circular tank… but it’s just an aquarium. If you do go in… try to catch a Q&A at the top of the tank and meet Myrtle the 100 year old Turtle.
  9. Newbury Street; If you came to Boston to shop, I actually feel bad for you. There are other cities more well-known for their shopping, and Boston has SO much more to offer. So if it’s all about the plastic for you… yes, Newbury Street is your go to. For the rest of us who can’t even afford to sneeze on this street… just avoid it at all costs. (Except the Fairy Shop which I love driving by!!! So many gnomes)
  10. Cheers!; Another tourist trap that locales avoid like the plague. Over-priced and done up specifically for the tourism-factor, but this isn’t what the bar looked like pre-TV show. They modified it for fans. SO many other bars in Boston to go to.

So that’s a typical Top 10 Tourism list you’ll find on almost any website. And some of them are legit. But here’s the real list:

  1. Boston Commons/Public Garden; Swan Boats , the Ducklings Statue , and Frog Pond.
  2. Fenway Park; Avoid driving in. See the Green Monstah and the CITGO sign.
  3. Boston Duck Tour;  Out of the Museum of Science.
  4. Union Oyster House near Fanueil Hall.
  5. Fanueil Hall Marketplace/Quincy Market; food, bars, street performers (great people watching!)
  6. Mike’s Pastries; Yes, there will be a line. Yes, it moves quickly. Yes, it’s worth it.
  7. Samuel Adams Brewery Tour; Boston is a major beer city- celebrate and see how it’s made! Free tour!!!
  8. USS Constitution Ship; Maybe it’s just because I come from a naval family, but I thought this was one of the most fascinating thing I’ve ever done in Boston. In Charlestown, right over the bridge.
  9. Pizzeria Regina; In the North End. The original. Worth any wait; SO delicious.
  10. The Finish Line of the Boston Marathon; Not sure I should need to explain why.

Fellow Bostonians or Boston tourists, please share below what your favorite Boston sites are!
