Mail

Mail service is provided by Canada Post. 

In many neighbourhoods, there is no actual post office. It is replaced by a Canada Post counter in a pharmacy.

 

Postal counters near the HEC Montréal Côte-Sainte-Catherine building:

Jean Coutu Pharmacy - 6200 Côte-des-Neiges – Mon-Fri 8 a.m. to 9 p.m. / Sat 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. / Sun 1-5 p.m.

Pharmaprix – 2635 Van Horne – Mon-Fri 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. / Sat 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. / Sun 10 a.m.-5 p.m.

 

Postal counters near the HEC Montréal Decelles building:

Pharmaprix – 5122 Côte-des-Neiges – Mon-Fri 8 a.m. to 9 p.m. / Sat 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. / Sun 10 a.m.-3 p.m.

 

For urgent letters and parcels, Canada Post offers the XpressPost line of services. You can also use companies such as UPS, Purolator or FedEx.

For non-urgent international letters and parcels, regular Canada Post services are sufficient.

Do not hesitate to request the follow-up and/or content insurance option for a valuable shipment. This is also true for parcels that your family might send you.
