Bass Pro Shops takes huge 145K sq ft retail lease

Bass Pro Shops -- the outdoor retailer known for its huge stores, lushly decorated interiors/exteriors and devoted following -- is coming to San Jose.

Don't care about outdoor gear? OK, but how does a 12-lane "underwater" bowling alley sound?

More on that later. First, the basics: The massive, 145,000-square-foot "Bass Pro Shops Outdoor World" store will be located at Almaden Ranch, a 350,000-square-foot retail complex at Almaden Expressway and Highway 85. Cupertino-based Hunter/Storm is the project's developer, and scoring an anchor tenant this big is a huge coup.

To put that size of store in comparison, the average Costco is around 150,000 square feet. Bass Pro Shops says the store will be completed in 2015.

Here's another metric: Jobs. A spokeswoman told me the store would bring up to 300 jobs (not counting seasonal or events employees).

This is only the third Bass Pro Shop in California (the others are in Manteca and Rancho Cucamonga), but the chain has a major presence in the South and Midwest and needs no introduction for many anglers and hunters. According to the company, 120 million people visit the chain's 77 stores in the U.S. and Candada, staying an average of 2.5 hours and driving more than 50 miles to get there. Once inside, shoppers can buy all kinds of camping, hunting, fishing and other outdoor merchandise.

The stores are also known for highly detailed, elaborate exterior and interior motifs -- and especially huge indoor aquariums and water features.

Bass Pro Shops spokeswoman Katie Mitchell told me the store will feature a couple out-of-the-box features, including "Uncle Buck's Fishbowl and Grill" -- that's a 12-lane bowling alley where you can bowl in an underwater theme, then sidle up to the grill for family-friendly dining.
