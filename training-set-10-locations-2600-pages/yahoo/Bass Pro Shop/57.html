Cabela's looks to add location in West Chester Ohio (Southwest Ohio near Cincinnati)

I wish it was here in Northern Kentucky, but this is a good thing for everyone in the surrounding Tristate area of Indiana, Ohio and Kentucky.

Basically according to documents filed with the planning commission, Cabela's plans to build and open a store in West Chester Ohio just slightly North of Cincinnati. Right now, the closest store is in Louisville roughly 100 miles away depending where you are in the Tristate.


http://www.fox19.com/story/25751594/cabelas-looks-to-add-location-in-west-chester

".....which would be located at the intersection of Liberty Way and Tylers Place Blvd. in West Chester, according to the proposal.The documents show a 74,000 square foot store located on an 18-acre lot. "

Right now, the closest store is in Louisville roughly 100 miles away depending where you are in the Tristate.

I live out to the east a few miles which makes the Polaris store far closer than the Louisville store. The one they're building will still be over 60 miles from where I live. And for other people in the tri-state it could be a lot farther.

To be honest I'm not all that crazy about the Cabela's stores I've been in. They don't have good prices on things IMO. And they don't have that great of a selection for guns. I suppose if I wanted to dress like the Duck Dynasty crowd it might be a good place to buy clothes. But there's already a Bass Pro shop in the area where they're building the new Cabelas and I don't see a lot of difference between them actually.

I don't think so many big box stores can be supported once the gun salesman in chief leaves office. Gun sales will likely go back to a more normal rate and that means it will be hard to keep so many shops open. There are other fairly big gun shops in that area too including Target World (which isn't that great IMO but it's pretty big). I think Vances in Columbus has those shops beat and I think that several gun shops near where I did live are better places to buy guns. I've gotten far better deals there. I do like the collection of really expensive guns at the Cabelas at Polaris. I can't afford them of course but maybe I'll find one I could buy some day. I have seen Sako rifles at Cabelas too which is something I haven't seen at other shops but I haven't really been to that Bass Pro shop near Forest Park. I've been to other Bass Pro shops though including one in Charlotte that was amazing. I guess it's actually an outlet too or they called it that when I was there more than a few years ago.
