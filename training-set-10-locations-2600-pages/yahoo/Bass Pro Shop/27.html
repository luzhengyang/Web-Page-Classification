    M A L L   O N   T H E   M E N D

    The Bass Pro Shops outlet is the anchor of Forest Fair Mall's $60 million redevelopment plan.

    Suburban Cincinnati may not be an outdoorsman's paradise, but don't tell that to the folks at the new Bass Pro Shops store at the Forest Fair Mall.

    The interactive retail outdoorsman chain opened its newest outlet in the former Parisian store Nov. 2 and has met with a stampede of customers--more than 100,000 in its first weekend alone.

    "We have more customers coming through our store than any other Bass Pro in history," spokesman Suzanne Wolfe said. "Things are phenomenal in here."

    That's a good adjective to describe the store's inventory, which ranges from $12 sleeves of golf balls to $10,000-plus powerboats. The store's furnishings are no less lavish-witness the 24,000-gallon aquarium placed in the middle of the first-floor showroom, the state-of-the-art laser arcade and shooting gallery, a cafeteria and a nature center stocked with books, videos and other gift ideas.

    "I think it's great," said Bob Koth of Fairfield. "Everybody loves it. I've been in here three times, and it's been so crowded that I haven't really been able to look around like I want to."

    But, thanks to a $250 gift certificate he won in a recent Bass Pro duck calling contest, Koth will do more than look around the 144,000-square foot facility. Such contests are one of several impromptu events offered during the store's three-week grand opening celebration, which wraps up Nov. 22. The store is still finalizing its calendar for next year, but plans are in the works to offer a fishing seminar in the spring and a hunting seminar next fall.

    "We believe in educating the public," Wolfe said. "We feel the more the public is aware of their surroundings, the better off they're going to be. We believe in conservation, and so a lot of our programs revolve around that.

    "We're not just here to sell the product, we're here to teach you how to use it so our customers can be more successful in their days out in the field."
