Know Before You Go: Toronto Pearson International Airport

  • Toronto Pearson is the busiest airport in Canada, with over 36 million passengers in 2013.
  • It has many great features for the air traveler, such as free Wi-Fi located throughout each terminal.
  • The Plaza Premium Lounge offers travelers a chance to relax and unwind in locations in both terminals. Rates for the lounge begin at $35 for two hours.
  • Needing some last minute electronics? A BestBuy Express vending machine is located in Terminal 1 and Terminal 3, after security.
  • The Sheraton Gateway Hotel has direct indoor access from Terminal 3 and easy access to Terminal 1. Rooms have double pane thermal windows to block outside noise.
  • Headed to a warm location? Leave that heavy winter coat behind with short term luggage storage and coat check. Simply pick up on your return flight home for a price of $2 a day - located at Samsonite in Terminal 1 and the Travel Store in Terminal 3.
  • When picking up arrivals, you can wait in the Cell Phone lot in your car for up to 45 minutes free of charge while you wait to pick up or drop off. Vehicles cannot be left unattended.
  • Craving a hot fresh burger made with only Ontario corn-fed beef? True Burger Co. features made to order burgers with your choice of toppings. You can also enjoy hand cut fries or an order of a Canadian favorite - loaded poutine.
  • Travel back in time and check out some dinosaurs in Terminal 1! Two incredible dinosaurs are on display courtesy of the Royal Ontario Museum.
  • Looking for some Canadian souvenirs? Canada Eh! offers an assortment of souvenir gift items, gourmet foods, toys, and travel accessories - located in Terminal 3, before security.

About Toronto Pearson International Airport

As the biggest and busiest hub in Canada, Toronto's Pearson International Airport welcomes some 32 million travelers each year. That's almost as many tourists as there are Toronto residents. When they arrive, most visitors are dazzled by the gorgeous architecture of the facility - the six-structure site is renowned for its state-of-the-art Terminal 3, which is designed in the style of a grand railway hall with massive, curved ceilings and expansive sky-lit spaces. Walk through to find a multitude of amenities, from boutique shops to fine dining spots.

Visitors can choose from a wide variety of ideally located Pearson International Airport hotels during their stay in Toronto. The facility is situated in the adjacent city of Mississauga, about 30 minutes by car from Toronto's downtown area. Many stay at the Crowne Plaza or Holiday Inn, which sit conveniently about 1 mile from the airport. To get to the heart of the city about 10 miles away, you can turn to a car rental or taxi. But Toronto also has an efficient and clean public transportation system that will take you all over the city by subway, streetcar and bus. In fact, the red line train picks up right outside of the airport.

Welcome to Toronto

The capital of Ontario - Toronto - is Canada's most populated city with 2.6 million residents. In fact, it's the fourth largest city in all of North America and a massive tourist destination known for its rich culture and vibrant nightlife. The hub of industry and finance attracts business people from around the globe, while vacationers come to explore the deep-rooted history of the locale. It was once the home of various Iroquois and Wyandot tribes until French settlement in the 1700s began the area's development into a major metropolis. In the 1960s, the city turned into a massive melting pot, attracting a diverse array of people with its welcoming immigration policies (today, more than 80 ethnicities live in harmony in this city).

Along with its unique heritage, the city is renowned for its stunning natural beauty coupled with its exquisite architecture. It's laid out along Lake Ontario's northwestern shore and is known for its massive expanses of green space all throughout, such as Rogue Park and South Humber Park. It's also home to a stunning multitude of colorful gardens and two different conservatories: Allan Gardens and Centennial Park.

Things to Do in Toronto

Whether you're visiting Toronto for business or just to have a good time, there's plenty to do to make your stay memorable and enjoyable. A tour of the city's engineering feats is a must - the mix of classical and contemporary architecture lining the streets of the many neighborhoods of Toronto's six districts makes for an intriguing display. Be sure to check out City Hall, CN Tower and Casa Loma.

While in town, take an afternoon to experience the locals in their natural habitat - at the Saint Lawrence Market, a bustling bazaar that serves as a place to meet with friends, shop for goods by local producers and simply enjoy a day at the market. Sports fans won't want to miss out on a visit to the Hockey Hall of Fame, while the adventurous at heart can hike through the rock terrain of the Scarborough Bluffs. When you've tuckered yourself out with a day of exploration, end the night with dinner and drinks at one of the favorite local establishments, such as Pizza Libretto, Siddhartha or Sen5es. Then, travelers can head back to their Toronto Pearson International Airport hotels for a night of rest and relaxation.
