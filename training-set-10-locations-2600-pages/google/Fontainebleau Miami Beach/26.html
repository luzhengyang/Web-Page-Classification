    Welcome to the Fontainebleau Resort Miami Beach
    Rising 1,000 feet above the ocean on Miami Beach, the non-smoking Fontainebleau Resort Miami Beach charms Priceline guests with its classic style and well-equipped rooms.

    Hotel Features
    Considered the classic Miami Beach hotel, this favorite of Frank Sinatra and the Rat Pack was built in 1954 by Morris Lapidus in Miami Modern architectural style. This 20-acre oceanfront hotel has 1,504 rooms divided among four buildings. Most accommodations have ocean or bay views, and some have balconies. Rooms are a tech-lover's dream, with large flat-panel HDTVs, 20-inch iMac computers and iPod docking stations. Free Wi-Fi and wired internet is included in the daily resort fee. Beds are designed with comfort in mind, with pillowtop mattresses, duvets and 400-thread-count Egyptian cotton sheets. Junior suites have separate sitting areas and sofa beds, and the larger suites add full kitchens and dining areas. Umbrellas, chairs, cabanas, jet skis and water sports equipment are available for rent. Other features include a fitness center, several hot nightclubs, the 40,000-square-foot Lapis spa, 12 restaurants including a steakhouse and award-winning Chinese restaurant, state-of-the-art conference and event facilities. Parking is available for an additional fee. The entire property is non-smoking.

    Hotel Location
    Situated on oceanfront Collins Avenue in the heart of Millionaire's Row, Fontainebleau is on Miami Beach. The sands and clubs of South Beach are two miles south of the property. Miami International Airport is a 20-minute drive away.

    Guest Favorites
    Priceline guests cite the "beautiful" lobby, "amazing beach" and "nice Miami atmosphere" as reasons to book the Fontainebleau Resort.
