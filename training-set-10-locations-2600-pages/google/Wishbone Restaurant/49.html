    • A Million Meals for Fifty Nine Years - NOT BAD!!
      Diner from Anchorage • jahnghalt • 1 review
      Lucky Wishbone has mostly everything required of a burger-n-chicken joint - ample parking, plenty of daylight, waiters with good attitudes, and a fast kitchen.
      In addition to the usual burgers, fries, and chicken a variety of grilled sandwiches, milkshakes (and malts) of many flavors, hamburger steaks are available.
      A tip, fries are "extra" - however, a full order will satisfy four diners. Another tip, a full order of coleslaw (which seems always to be made that day) will also feed four.
      "The Bone" has recently... Read more » raised it's price for a 1/4-pound halibut sandwich from $7 to a whopping $8 - to my knowledge the only halibut sandwich in town for under $10. The Cod Sandwich is still $5.
      A note on execution - the kitchen is often so responsive that it seems like they deliver before the order! Fries are so hot that forks will protect fingers for a minute or two.
      Finally, it seems that another review(er) of 6/18/2014 doesn't get that a living, breathing entity like a restaurant can sometimes screw up. This does not invalidate nearly 60 years of success - the table right next to theirs may well have had the same fine fast meal that I've had nearly 100 times over thirty-odd years.

    • An Anchorage Institution
      Likes it
      Diner from Alaska • Da Dornta • 13 reviews
      I have been going to the Lucky Wishbone ever since I was a small child in the 1980's. My mother used to frequent the place back in the 60's when she was in Highschool. I always get the same thing: Cheeseburger Deluxe with a 1/2 order of fries.
      While some may argue that other places around town have a better burger -- I disagree. I like my hamburgers fairly basic. I don't like a ton of things on them, and the 'Bone delivers.
      The patties seem to almost fall apart because they are so tender and juicy, the buns are steamed, and the lettuce... Read more » is nicely chopped.
      I always tell people new to the Lucky Wishbone that their french fries are almost identical to McDonalds -- and I've had several people agree. In some ways they are almost better. Unlike at Mc'Ds, you never will get an order of cold soggy fries!
      The Lucky Wishbone is an Anchorage institution, and the it's history is all over the walls for everyone to see. From what my mother told me, the Lucky Wishbone was the first place in Anchorage to have a drive up window. It really is a special place (and their parking lot reflects this!)
      The staff have everything down to a science, and even during a lunch rush you'll never usually wait more than 10 minutes for a table. You don't pay your waitress, but a dedicated cashier when you're done. This frees up the waitstaff to seat people and deliver food.
      I think it's great that Anchorage has a honest-to-goodness real diner. Unlike another "Diner" in Anchorage, this one has over 40+ years of history. The whole "diner vibe" really only works when the place actually is old. You just can't replicate that experience in a new shiny chrome replica.
      Do yourself a favor, head down to the 'Bone and get a milkshake, burger and fries and enjoy the odd assortment of elderly, young, professional and blue collar people happily munching away! « Read less
