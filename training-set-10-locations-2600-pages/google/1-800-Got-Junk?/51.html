Need To Brighten Up Dull Content? Take a Cue From 1-800-GOT-JUNK

March 4th, 2013 | Posted by Mary Yang in Community Management/Engagement | E-Government | Social Media | Uncategorized | Usability | Weblogs

By Mary Yang, Senior Communications Manager, GovDelivery

There is content available out on the web that is garbage. Seriously! The website for 1-800-GOT-JUNK? features information on the company’s junk removal services in a way that attracts potential customers that never thought they needed help taking out the trash.

A recent post on ragan.com provided tips for companies looking to emulate 1-800-GOT-JUNK and transform “boring” business content into content that excites consumers. Here’s our take for government organizations on how to use similar concepts to refresh communications for citizens. These tips can help your organization think of digital messages in different ways, attract new visitors and improve citizen engagement.

Create content for citizens that don’t think they need you.
Frame messages in a way that educates citizens that don’t already know how your agency’s services are relevant to their lives. For example, a blog post listing the most important things to consider before digging in a residential yard could appeal to individuals thinking about starting a landscaping project while pointing them to the number for a government call center on utility line information. Focus on what information is considered valuable by your target audience, then create content and gear information on your services to meet those needs.

Link trending topics to information on your services.
1-800-GOT-JUNK? offers tips via their blog on de-cluttering and garage sales, and shares how their company cares about environmental sustainability. Keeping an eye on local events and what is trending on social media can help trigger ideas on interesting topics that you can tie back to your agency. Post about trends and timely themes, like 1-800-GOT-JUNK’s feature on helping people with New Year’s resolutions and article relating junk to Hurricane Sandy .Then, use Facebook, Twitter and other social media to inform followers about newly available content.

Create an interesting attention grabber.
1-800-GOT-JUNK? features a counter showing the number of pounds of trash the service has prevented from being dumped in landfills.

Counter on 1/9/13

Use a highly visible widget on your organization’s online platforms to share fun stats on other citizens gaining real value from government services. For example, an agency website could show a count of the number of licenses provided from a newly opened department office or a ticker of minutes saved with a new online request form. Analytics can be leveraged to discover what tactics are getting the most attention from web users and fine-tune content and online visual tools.

Connect with readers through emotion and help solve real problems.
1-800-GOT-JUNK’s “Not Another Junky Blog” frames information on their business in a way that connects to the emotional release people feel when they get rid of unwanted stuff. The blog also tries to offer information on topics that may not necessarily gain the company business, like how to run a garage sale, but does offer a form of value to customers that may then remember the company in the future. What do your stakeholders value? What information can your organization offer that relates to your citizens’ values? By offering readily available information that citizens find useful, entertaining, and personal, they will be more likely to think of your organization and engage with your services in the future.

If 1-800-GOT-JUNK? can turn man’s trash into blog content treasure, any government agency can turn services and information once considered “boring” into compelling web content. Communications that are created for the widest possible audience with timely updates, attention-grabbing details, and emotional appeal will reach the most citizens and ultimately maximize the value your organization can provide to your community.

What is your organization doing to recharge and refresh communications in the New Year?
