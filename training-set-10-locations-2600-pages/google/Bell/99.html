BY ALYSSA DURANTY, SCOTT SCHWEBKE and SALVADOR HERNANDEZ / STAFF WRITERS

Published: Nov. 3, 2014 Updated: 6:29 p.m.

SANTA ANA â€“ Police today identified the suspected driver in the Halloween hit-and-run that killed three teenagers as a 31-year-old Orange County man with a history of traffic offenses.

Jaquinn Ramone Bell has a previous conviction for driving under the influence and hit and run charges in August, court records show.

Bell was arrested around 11:30 a.m. Sunday near a Stanton motel, where he was staying with his two children, on suspicion of felony hit-and-run causing death, Santa Ana Police Chief Carlos Rojas said during a news conference.

RELATED: See what the public is saying about the case on social media.

Twins Lexi and Lexandra Perez Huerta and their friend Andrea Gonzalez, all 13, were killed around 6:45 p.m. Friday when police say a 2011 Honda CRV struck the group of costumed girls in a crosswalk in the 1400 block of East Fairhaven Avenue while they were trick-or-treating.

Investigators say Bell was driving west Friday night on Fairhaven Avenue with his two children in the Honda CRV, which was not stolen but also was not registered to Bell, when he hit the girls â€œand did not attempt to stop or render aid to the children that he hit,â€ Rojas said.

Officers later found the SUV about 800 yards away parked in a shopping center parking lot.

Several witnesses provided information to police officers, who were able to track Bell to a Motel 6 at 7450 Katella Ave. in Stanton after going to â€œquite a few different locationsâ€ where they had set up 24-hour surveillance before his arrest, Rojas said.

Police would not comment on how Bell got to the motel, but they think he was not living there.

â€œNumerous witnesses were able to give us good information and leads,â€ Rojas said. â€œThere are a lot of facets to this investigation, and we want to make sure we investigate as thoroughly as possible.â€

Santa Ana police took Bell into custody Sunday with his mother and half-sister, who later were released. His son, 17, and daughter, 14, initially were placed in protective custody before being released to a family member.

CRIMINAL PAST

Bell has a history of reckless driving in Orange County, according to court records.

Bell pleaded guilty Aug. 4 to hit-and-run with property damage, driving under the influence and two counts of child endangerment in an incident that occurred Aug. 1.

He was sentenced to three years probation, seven days in jail and a first-offender alcohol program.
