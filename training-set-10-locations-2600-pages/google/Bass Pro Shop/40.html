Bass Pro has North Palm Beach location on the line

By Joe Capozzi

Palm Beach Post Staff Writer

Palm Beach County commissioners granted zoning approval today for a World Wide Sportsman fishing store in suburban Palm Beach Gardens.

Commissioners hope the high-end fishing store, operated by Bass Pro, will inject economic life into the vacant Carl’s Furniture plaza on U.S. 1 just north of PGA Boulevard, which sits in an unincorporated county pocket north of Palm Beach Gardens.

Worldwide Sportsman – which hopes to open the new store next year — sells saltwater sports fishing equipment, boats, apparel and camping gear. Two other Florida locations of the Springfield, Mo.-based chain are in Islamorada in the keys and in the panhandle town of Destin.

Plans call for renovating the 6-acre site on the east side of U.S. 1 for retail (58,500 square feet), warehouse (5,000 square feet) and boat sales (5,000 square feet) space. Specific site plans still face further hearings by the commission.

But commissioners, sitting as the Zoning Commission, unanimously gave the project a key approval it needed to move forward. The commission included one main condition, which is to allow weekend deliveries to the store only on Saturdays.

Carl’s Furniture did not have weekend deliveries when it operated on the site, but county officials recommended the Saturday delivery for the fishing store after meeting with nearby property owners who agreed with it as long as the deliveries are confined to 9 a.m.-6 p.m.

“That’s an integral part of their business,’’ Commissioner Hal Valeche said of the Saturday deliveries.

Commissioners rejected a request from one homeowner who wanted the fishing store to help maintain a private canal just east of a wall at the end of the site and west of Hidden Key, the closest residential neighborhood.

Carl’s Furniture never was required to maintain the canal, so World Wide Sportsman shouldn’t have to, either, commissioners said.

“All of our operations are west of the wall and that is pursuant to the requests of the neighbors,’’ said Harvey Oyer, an attorney representing Bass Pro.

“We probably would have been delighted to put the boats that we are selling in the water behind our property, but we were told in no uncertain terms by the neighbors, don’t do that. So we, of course, are not doing that.’’

The store’s boats will be displayed on the site and delivered to a prospective buyer’s home.

After the meeting, Valeche said he’s optimistic that World Wide Sportsman will help provide a much-needed economic boost to the commercial corridor on U.S. 1 north of PGA Boulevard.

“Going from a vacant property to a worldwide chain that’s recognized as a very high-quality retailer is going to be a complete turnaround for that little part of U.S. 1,’’ Valeche said.
