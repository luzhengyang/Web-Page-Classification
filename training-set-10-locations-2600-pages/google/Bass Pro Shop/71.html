Crystal Palace to make way for region’s first Bass Pro Shop

PATRICIA BROOKS ARENBURG BUSINESS REPORTER
Published July 31, 2014 - 1:06pm
Last Updated July 31, 2014 - 4:53pm

The fun is soon coming to an end at Crystal Palace.

As of Sept. 1, the indoor amusement site outside Moncton will be no more, followed by the closure of the Ramada Plaza Crystal Palace Hotel and Convention Centre and McGinnis Landing Restaurant on Oct. 31.

These changes will make way for an 110,000-square-foot Bass Pro Shops store specializing in hunting, fishing and outdoor gear.

“As difficult a decision as it is, it’s a business opportunity,” Brian MacMullin, general manager for Champlain Place, said in an interview Thursday.

Bass Pro has 87 locations in the U.S. and Canada, and this will be its fourth Canadian store.

It’s expected to draw customers from five to six hours away to stay and shop in the region, MacMullin said, and is a “nice fit” with the Champlain Place shopping complex, owned by Cadillac Fairview Corp. Ltd.

The move is also “a re-affirmation of the strength of the Greater Moncton and Southeast New Brunswick economy that they’re willing to come and make a long-term commitment to be in this market,” MacMullin said.

Employees of the businesses slated for closure will receive severance and customers who have booked rooms or events at the hotel will be contacted, Cadillac Fairview said in a news release issued Thursday.

Owned by the Ontario Teachers’ Pension Plan, Cadillac Fairview says it has nearly 43 million square feet of commercial space and has a portfolio worth $24 million.

When asked whether poor performance at Crystal Palace led to the decision to close, MacMullin said: “It wouldn’t be a fair statement to say that it’s losing money.”

“Obviously, the Maritime Fun Group wouldn’t have shown an interest in the business opportunity if they didn’t believe that it was an opportunity for them as well.”

The rides from Crystal Palace have been bought by the Maritime Fun Group, which includes Magic Mountain Waterpark in Moncton, and Shining Waters Family Fun Park, Sandspit and Burlington Amusement Park, all of Prince Edward Island.

“The truth is we don’t know at this point” what the company will do with the equipment, John Jelley, general manager of Magic Mountain, said in an interview Thursday.

“This opportunity just came up very recently for us and we’re still looking through all sorts of different options to try to decide where that equipment will land.”

The new Bass Pro location is scheduled to open in 2015, and construction is expected to begin shortly after the hotel, convention centre and restaurant close in October, MacMullin said.

“This area offers some of Canada’s best hunting and fishing opportunities,” Johnny Morris, founder of Bass Pro Shops, said on the company’s website.

The statement on the website also describes the riches of the Maritime provinces, including “11,000 kilometres of saltwater coastline, 250 rivers, 6,500-plus lakes and more than 10,000 kilometres of trails (and) world-famous fishing and hunting.”

“Some of the best anglers head to Canada to fish everything from salmon to shark; from brook trout to small-mouth bass. Trophy black bear, moose, white-tailed deer and waterfowl can be found in the area’s pristine forests, secluded marshes and coastal woods.”

The new location will also feature large indoor aquariums and water features, historic photos, wildlife mounts and a boat showroom, the website states.

“This regional location is very accessible and will allow us to … better serve the many sportsmen and women as well as the many visitors to the Moncton area each year. We are very excited to be a part of this vibrant community,” Morris said on the website.
