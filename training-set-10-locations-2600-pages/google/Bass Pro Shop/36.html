    I hate this place.
    My boyfriend drags me here first thing in the morning, in between meals, and at night. He is willing to go anywhere in the vicinity, so we can casually "stop by to pick up a few things" afterwards.

    All jokes aside, this place is huge and has something for everyone. Boats, hunting, fishing, camping, clothing, etc. I will have to say the highlights are the large aquarium and fake animals in the middle. I spend a lot of time with these animals while my boyfriend wanders around and looks at every single item in the store, deciding each one is the next EVOLUTIONARY GAME CHANGER to his fishing life. Sometimes when I've lost interest in the fishes and have exhausted all my stupid selfie poses with the moose, he even let's me pick out a shiny lure. One time he even got me a World's Fishing Network shirt. His next move was towards the camo bikini, so I was quick to catch on.

    This is the TOYS R US for the lover of the outdoors. If your man does not already know about BPS, do not- I repeat- do NOT introduce him to this place unless you share the same love for weighted fishing nets and Duck Dynasty kayaks. You want to sit and watch him feel the weight of a Shimano Teramar Inshore for 30 minutes?

    The bf always finds what he is looking for (and more) and there are always helpful workers walking around offering guidance and/or trophy fish stories. Despite hitting my boredom limit 45 minutes in, hours at the expansive BPS means my boyfriend is happy.

    This also means we probably get to eat sushi later and also stop by the outlet mall. It's the ultimate love/hate relationship.

    "Yay, I'm a fan."

    Holy geez. This place is like Disneyland and a train wreck simultaneously.

    Biggest dang sporting goods shop that I've been in in recent memory. Specializing in fishing, hunting, and camping. Given this one is in Ft. Myers on the Gulf, it's mainly fishing. However the entire upstairs is camping and hunting, complete with its own gun shop. Enough guns here to put the Lee County Sheriff's Dept. confiscation vault to shame.

    Everything one would ever need to go fishing - from lines, lures, reels, poles, nets, chum containers, wet & dry suits, bait, actual boats (there is a boat sales room) - you name it, the list can go on and on.

    There is also a gift section, book section (huge, like shopping Amazon or Borders in the Sports sections), apparel section, and (here's where it starts to get like Disneyland), an aquarium, "rides" for the kids, and a huge wood burning fireplace - the other place in the store where lots of Kodak moments were happening. I kid you not. The amount of people taking pictures in this store with their kids in front of things was unbelievable.

    This store is definitely a destination store. You can spend hours in here. I know we did and all were were looking for was the fishing stuff.

    The prices were not outrageous.  A lot of this fighing gear we were looking at was priced at parity with the same items on the internet.

    This store has a full class schedule. Some that I noted: Archery for Kids, Concealed Weapons Class, Throwing a Cast Net, Intro to Salt Water Fishing, Fly Fishing Basics, Casting Basics, Handgun Seminar....the list goes on. Also on the schedule was fish feeding at the aquarium every Saturday at 2pm.

    One other thing that impressed me about this store is that for every section there were products and lines just for women and kids. In the fishing section there were pink and purple poles that were lighter and shorter for women. Special hunting and camping gear especially for women and kids And, of course, tons of clothing for women.

    There is a restaurant attached - The Islamorada Fish Company - in case you're one of those to come from afar and make a day of it.  We did not try it but it looks like some others Yelpers have.

    Bass Pro is even open on Thanksgiving from 8am - 6pm!!  Probably having demostrations where you go and hunt you're own turkey in the back parking lot then take it inside to learn how to depluck and cook it.  All while browsing the Cookware section so you walk out with a ton of cool purchases which you didn't really need.

    And one last thing - this store is People Watching at its finest (ding ding!!).

    This is an amazing store if you're into outdoorsy stuff, and I don't think it's the redneck in me coming out either.
