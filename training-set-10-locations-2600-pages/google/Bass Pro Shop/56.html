Bass Pro Shops unveils plans for Outpost store


The 80,000-square-foot Outpost store will be the first of its kind in Southwest Florida for Bass Pro Shops.

Photo provided / Bass Pro Shops
By Justine Griffin
Published: Monday, July 7, 2014 at 5:37 p.m.
Last Modified: Monday, July 7, 2014 at 5:37 p.m.

SARASOTA COUNTY - Bass Pro Shops officially announced it will open an 80,000-square-foot Outpost store here in 2016.

The store — a smaller format compared with the previously reported 140,000-square-foot Outdoor World — will still be a first for Southwest Florida.

The megastore outdoor chain, which sells fishing, camping, hunting, boating and other gear, will anchor a 260,000-square-foot mixed-use retail center called “Fruitville Commons” at the northeast quadrant of Interstate 75 and Fruitville Road, several miles south of the Mall at University Town Center, according to an announcement issued late Monday.

The store will be the company's 14th in Florida, and the second one in development on the Gulf Coast. Bass Pro Shops broke ground on a 150,000-square-foot Outdoor World in April near Westfield Group's Brandon Town Center.

The Herald-Tribune had confirmed Bass Pro Shops' entrance into the Sarasota-Bradenton market in May, after the annual International Council of Shopping Centers conference in Las Vegas.

Bass Pro officials say the store will attract customers from nearly 50 miles away, noting that more than 100,000 vehicles will pass by the store each day from Interstate 75 and other thoroughfares.

“Having owned the property for more than 30 years, we are extremely excited that Bass Pro Shops has chosen our site for the home of their latest complex,” said David Walters, trustee of the Walters' Trust, in the Monday statement. “The concept of a Bass Pro Shops Outpost store is exactly what was envisioned when the mixed-use Fruitville Initiative was conceived.”

Fruitville Commons will be built on one of six parcels in the area that form the so-called “Fruitville Initiative,” a 420-acre parcel near Fruitville Road owned by several property investors and Sarasota County.

Sarasota County officials are working with Fruitville Initiative developers to draw a variety of employers to the area but still preserve the rural environment near the highway.

Manatee County-based Benderson Development Co. also has expressed interest in building a business park near the center, which would include assembly warehouses and research areas.

Originally, Bass Pro Shops was looking to open a store inside the Benderson-owned complex around the Mall at University Town Center, at the junction of University Parkway and I-75.

The Sarasota County Bass Pro Shops store will have an Uncle Buck's Fishbowl and Grill.

The 20,000-square-foot, nautical-themed restaurant will be within the store and offer 12 lanes for bowling.

“Having the opportunity to build a super outdoor store directly fronting I-75 will allow us to bring Bass Pro Shops low prices and famous friendly, expert service to serve the many sportsmen, women as well as the visitors to this area each year,” said Johnny Morris, founder of Bass Pro Shops, in the Monday statement. “It is an honor to be part of an exciting development such as Fruitville Commons and the history-rich Sarasota community.”

The store also will likely have indoor ponds filled with fish found in this region and with taxidermy displays of local animals.

The Outpost store is expected to employ 200 full- and part-time workers when it opens.

The fishing areas and restaurant features make Bass Pro Shops less of a store and more of an experience, analysts say, which is a huge part of the chain's mass appeal.

The company is expanding, and quickly.

Bass Pro Shops announced plans to open an 80,000-square-foot store in Gainesville, its 13th in Florida.

Other stores in Jacksonville and Daytona are expected to open soon.
