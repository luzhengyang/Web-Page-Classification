Wells Fargo lends Wyoming Valley CYC a hand

Volunteer project builds two playgrounds in Wilkes-Barre

Last updated: October 18. 2014 5:37PM - 1982 Views

WILKES-BARRE — More than 100 volunteers gathered on Saturday to help rejuvenate a city block to benefit area youth.


The project was spearheaded and funded by Wells Fargo Bank with the cooperation of the Wyoming Valley Catholic Youth Center and several local businesses.


Mayor Tom Leighton, Bishop Joseph Bambera and Greg Collins, president of Wells Fargo Northeastern Pennsylvania, attended the event. Jack McNulty, from state Rep. Eddy Pashinski’s office; Mark Soprano, executive director of the CYC; Bill Jones, president of the United Way of Wyoming Valley; and John Maday, from the Greater Wilkes-Barre Chamber of Commerce, were also in attendance.


Leighton praised the efforts of Wells Fargo’s staff and management at the ribbon-cutting ceremony where the CYC received a check for $80,000 from the bank. The bank donated to a large improvement in the city that benefits everyone, he said.


The project is the result of the strong commitment Wells Fargo has to improving the “quality of life” for the community in Wilkes-Barre and all of Northeastern Pennsylvania, Collins said.


After visiting the CYC about two months ago when he was offered a tour of the nonprofit facility by Soprano, Collins said his “eyes were opened.”


Some of the play areas just looked “blighted,” he said. He contacted Kevin Engleman, who oversees the bank’s Wilkes-Barre area locations, to come up with a plan to help.


“We began to strategize and act as a catalyst for improvement,” Collins said.


They recruited bank employees, their friends and family members to volunteer to help with the work. And using the bank’s networks, they were able to hire local companies to provide pro bono services which Soprano estimates worth about another $25,000.


Saturday’s work included:


• Construction of two state-of-the-art playground areas, one for children ages 2 to 5 and one for children ages 5 and up;


• Painting of childcare areas inside the CYC;


• Landscaping, including trimming shrubs and trees, weeding and cleaning of flower beds, and clearing and cleaning of walkway into playground area;


• Painting of a 20-foot long, 8-foot wide mural titled “Celebrating our children…Believing in their future.”


“This is really unheard of,” Soprano said. “We did not have an agenda when we reached out to Wells Fargo. They did this on their own.


“Bishop Banbera was flabbergasted at the generosity.”


The CYC also sponsored a community festival with children’s activities, music, food and other activities as part of event.


“We are very proud of how it all turned out. It was a successful day,” Collins said.
