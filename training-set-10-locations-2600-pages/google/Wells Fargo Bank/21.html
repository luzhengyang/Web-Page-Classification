Watch as Lance Gross shares a lithograph of the first African American congressmen in the United States Congress and tells their story of struggle and progress. Thirteen of the twenty-two were born into slavery and championed the rights of all Americans to serve the U.S. government.

Union Soldier, 1864

Artist Unknown

The Kinsey Collection holds several photographs including this hand-colored tintype of a Union Soldier during the Civil War. Shortly after the Emancipation Proclamation, African American troops were allowed to fight as Union soldiers. Roughly 180,000 African Americans served as Union soldiers during the Civil War.

The Boss, 2006

The Boss, 2006

Bisa Butler

This quilt, created by the artist Bisa Butler, depicts the strength of an African American matriarch in the 1800s, while also challenging certain stereotypes. Butler earned her MFA at Howard University, where the rich culture of black college life influenced her development as an artist. At Howard, Butler began to merge her love of fabric with artistic expression, creating vibrant quilts that depict black life.

Guest House, 1950

Guest House, 1950

Claude Clark

Guest House is an oil on board that Shirley Kinsey hand-selected because it was reminiscent of her Southern childhood. Art pieces like this encourage the Kinseys to continue on their reflective journey of art acquisition and education. The artist, Claude Clark, became recognized in the 1960s, and more of his work can be seen at the Smithsonian in Washington D.C. and the De Young Museum in San Francisco.

About The Kinsey Collection/Wells Fargo Collaboration

2013 marks the 150th anniversary of the Emancipation Proclamation. With this in mind, Wells Fargo worked closely with the Kinsey family to create a unique celebratory experience that honors the progress of African Americans, not just for a single month, but for the entire year. The Kinsey Collection includes treasured pieces of history and art that commemorate the artistic, historic, and cultural contributions and progress of African Americans prior to and following the Emancipation Proclamation.

Wells Fargo embraces the arts as a voice for history and culture. We are excited to present The Kinsey Collection: Shared Treasures of Bernard and Shirley Kinsey - Where Art and History Intersect as a way to highlight these important untold stories involving the rich history of African Americans and their contribution to American history.
