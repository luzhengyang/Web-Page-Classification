Washington state robber used baby doll to rob Wells Fargo bank

Jared Bestrom, 37, was caught with loads of cash flying out of his pockets by Spokane Police officers Thursday after using a toy wrapped in a blanket to hold up a Wells Fargo in Spokane, Wash.

BY Nicole Hensley

Wads of cash scattered out a Washington state robber’s pockets as he scurried right past a cop while fleeing a holdup.

It took only four minutes for Spokane Police officers to catch up with Jared Bestrom, 37, after he handed a note over to a Wells Fargo teller demanding cash.

Witnesses such as Bill Pauloski then watched police surround Bestrom shouting, “Get down, get down, get your hands up,’ as money fluttered down the street.

“Holy moly,” Paulouski told KREM-TV after seeing the arrest outside his window.

Bank employees initially believed he was carrying what appeared to be a baby wrapped in a blanket while robbing the establishment for an undisclosed amount of cash, Capt. Dave Richards told the TV station.

But after Bestrom stashed the blanket under a cover, it was later revealed to be only a toy.

Richards also described Bestrom as having “some fluctuating emotions from anger to sadness” before being booked into Spokane County Jail on robbery charges.
