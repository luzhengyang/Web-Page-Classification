BS Fees on College Checking Helpful to 6 out of 6 people

When I went to college I decided to establish a Wells Fargo account since they had an ATM in the student center. During orientation there was a representative in the student center that was signing people up. She was a fast talker and never really explained the terms to me, instead just giving me a free iPod speaker (which was cheaply made and way too heavy on the treble, I might add) and placing it in my Wells Fargo bag, which just so happened to contain a thick booklet full of terms. All she did was explain how to set up 2 monthly direct deposits to avoid a monthly account maintenance fee.

At the time I applied, I had no credit history. I was just beginning my freshman year so loans were not yet factoring in, and I did not have any credit cards. I was still sore from my rejection from the Capital One Journey card (which has horrible customer service - I strongly recommend the Discover IT or Citi Forward if you're looking for a student card. I have reviews on here for both of them), so when the representative told me I could apply for a student credit card I was skeptical. I asked if I'd get approved with no credit history. She assured me that I would and I had nothing to worry about. All I did was sign a paper, and I never had to say anything about my income or anything which seemed kind of shady in retrospect. Eventually I received mail from Wells Fargo regarding my application. It was a denial letter. The two reasons for my denial? Insufficent credit history and not enough money in my bank account. I guess Wells Fargo uses all of their stupid fees to pay for detectives, because I sure couldn'tve told them that one!I I don't know what they were expecting, considering I had just joined and was a student who was trying to establish credit.

The next thing that went awry was when I went to the ATM at 7-11 to check my balance. In my experiences with my local credit union I've never been charged a fee to simply check my balance so I thought nothing of it. After seeing my balance, I decided to take out $20. I thought nothing of this until I checked my statement and saw $5 in fees (for using an out of network ATM) ON TOP OF the surcharge that the ATM charges you.  $27.50 to take out $20?!? No thank you.

I recognize that this fee was my fault (gotta read terms and conditions like a hawk), but I still think it's ridiculous. Sometimes stuff comes up and you need cash, so it's unreasonable to pay an extra fee.

Denying me for credit, ridiculous ATM fees,and the fact that I had to jump through more hoops than a dolphin at Sea World to keep my college checking account free of maintenance fees have led me to once choice: cancelling on principal. I'm signing up for the local credit union the moment I get to school this year!

Only stick with Wells Fargo if you have either: large balances, or an affinity for getting nickled and dimed to death. (I fully recognize that if you have lots of assets with them Wells can be great, but if you're a broke college kid like me, LOOK ELSEWHERE!)

Company Overview

Headquartered in San Francisco, Wells Fargo Bank delivers banking, insurance, investments, mortgage, consumer finance, and commercial finance. Wells Fargo reaches consumers through over 9,000 branches, 12,000 ATMs, plus convenient online and mobile banking services. Wells Fargo Bank is a nationwide, diversified financial services company that has provided consumers with banking products for over 150 years. With $1.2 trillion in assets, Wells Fargo is known as one of the four biggest banks in the United States.

Founded in 1852, Wells Fargo is widely recognized by its logo of a six-horse Concord Coach that was widely used to rush important business for their customers. This dedication is a continued standard for current customers.
