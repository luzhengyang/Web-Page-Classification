    We attempt to avoid shipping through FedEx, especially when it involves items crossing the border...but when push comes to shove, if it's the only way to get my parcel, I'll give in. That said, unbeknownst to me, our recent order of our wedding album from a company in the States, shipped via FedEx.

    In addition to leaving a notice slip wedged in our door (we don't have a mailbox), we were also dinged with a $25.00 duty fee, AND unless we were going to be home during business hours the next day, a pickup was required half-way across town.
    (This location is located in an industrial area of the south east - quite a bit out of the way.) Thankfully, picking it up was seamless (and I was willing to pay the extra fees - It IS my wedding album, after all) the staff were friendly and efficient, and as we arrived closer to 17:30 (as opposed to closing time - 18:00) the wait was virtually non-existent. We were also thankful for a phone call at 8:00 informing us that it was indeed at the facility for pickup - so we didn't waste a trip.

    While we wouldn't make FedEx our shipping company of choice, when it's all said and over with - I got my package and it was seemingly well-cared for en route - With all of the snow, I was also particularly grateful that this time it wasn't left to sit on the porch (even if it did mean the inconvenience of picking it up.)
