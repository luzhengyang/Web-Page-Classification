By Jessica Polizzotti
Boston Local Expert

Save to My Lists+

Boston’s harbor-side location allows easy access to the aphrodisiac of the sea-oysters. Harvesting oysters happens all around Boston with many oyster "farmers" raising oysters in a sustainable way. You’d think the city would be overrun with oyster bars, but it is not. There are few raw bars in the city, and of those few even fewer gems. With similar pricing, your choice really depends on the atmosphere you crave, if reservations should be made, and if there is more to what you want than oysters alone. 

10 Atlantic Fish
A beloved part of the Boston scene since the late 1970s, Atlantic Fish sports an updated atmosphere these days in other words, the original's galley look was traded in for a more polished scene of stained wood, pendant lights, coffered ceiling and crisp white linens. The menu, though hasn't changed all that much over the years (Hey, why mess with a good thing!), and Atlantic's kitchen continues to serve up timeless raw bar classics like shrimp cocktail, iced Littleneck clams and Blue Point oysters. Patio dining is available. (617-267-4000)

9 Citizen Public House and Oyster Bar
Citizen Public House is something different on the oyster scene. This is a tavern complete with fried pickles and gourmet pb&j, it is not the place you'd expect to turn out delicious oyster. Somehow this combo really works. Located behind Fenway Park it perfect for a pre or post game snack. (617-450-9000)

8 Abe & Louie's
Known as a steakhouse, Abe & Louis has a little secret-an amazing raw bar. Sending out fantastic platters of oyster, clams, crab and lobster, it is a wonder anyone makes it to the main course. Exquisitely presented, the raw bar combinations are brought table side to be admired. As good to eat as they are to look at, Abe & Louis has one fantastic raw bar. (617-536-6300)

7 Gaslight
Gaslight is not technically an oyster bar; in fact it is a French Brasserie with an impressive selection of raw bar treats. Gaslight is the place to go when dinner calls for more than oysters alone. A casually elegant interior is swanky without being stuffy. The lively bar scene is the perfect place to sit and order your raw bar essentials, or opt for a patio seat; with warming stations you can enjoy the night well after summer ends and much in advance of when it begins. (6174220224)

6 Union Oyster House
Union Oyster House is a taste of Boston history. Opened in 1826, and holding the title of America's oldest restaurant, Union Oyster House still offers one mean oyster bar. Just outside Faneuil Hall Marketplace, Union Oyster House is the perfect place to start your tour. Sit at the first floor raw bar to devour your oysters, or step into the past and explore the rest of the place. Cozy booths line this relic and the smaller scale helps transport diners to a time when things were not so “big". (617-227-2750)

5 East Coast Grill & Raw Bar
East Coast Grill boasts some mighty fine oysters. The Asian oysters are not meant for oyster purists, but are a special treat for anyone who likes to spice things up. East Coast Grill offers a raw bar platter that meets most standards: heavy on the oysters, light on the clams, with just enough shrimp and crab to balance things out, you can share it or make it your meal. The mango mignonette is simply amazing. Set in Inman Sq, East Coast Grill offers many items to tickle your taste buds including some mean BBQ, their infamous "Hell Nights", and a brunch with a really big, really fun Bloody Mary bar. (617-491-6568)

4 Legal Harborside
Legal Harborside Floor 1 offers one of the best ocean views around. Floor to ceiling windows adorn the harbor-side of this excellent oyster joint. A raw bar sits near the center with seating that allows diners to watch the water change. In typical Legal fashion, Legal Harborside serves up super fresh oysters at reasonable prices. Not up for bar side seating, no problem Legal Harborside, Floor 1, has many tables, most perfectly placed to enjoy the view. (617-477-2900)

3 B&G Oysters
B&G is a South End treasure by famed chef Barbara Lynch. Located below street level, B&G boasts a simple setting that allows diners to focus on the fabulous food. Oysters are the star here, so order up. The enclosed patio takes you away from the hustle and bustle of the neighborhood and transports you into a garden sanctuary. The quality entrees are expensive, but well worth the splurge. (617-423-0550)

2 Island Creek Oyster Bar
Island Creek Oyster Bar does not exclusively sell Island Creek oysters (from Duxbury, MA). They open their menu to many other oysters from coast to coast. With such a large selection, this is an oyster oasis. The newcomer to the seafood scene, Island creek has quickly secured its place as one of the best oyster bars around. Island Creek does not sell oysters exclusively (although they certainly offer a lot of options), and is a place worth eating more than oysters. (617-532-5300)

1 Neptune Oyster
Neptune Oyster is the oyster lover's oyster bar. Neptune's selection of east and west coast oysters are impressive. With sleek décor this no frills oyster bar is the perfect spot to sit and sip champagne while dozens of these fresh mollusks arrive before your eyes. The oysters may be amazing, but Neptune does not disappoint with its entrees either. An award winning lobster roll, and seasonal specialties take this oyster bar beyond basic. (617-742-3474)

About Jessica Polizzotti

Born and raised in Boston, Jessica knows her city like the back of her hand.  Always looking to uncover something new, Jessica keeps up on the latest and greatest Boston offerings.  Although Jessica has been a world traveler since her teens, her favorite trip may always be her Italian honeymoon.  Living in a city that allows her to experience a change in seasons equals an opportunity to enjoy a shift in activities. As the old New England cliché says, “If you don’t like the weather, just wait a minute.”
