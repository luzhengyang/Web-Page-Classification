Union Oyster House: Classic Seafood Near Park Lane Seaport

February 24, 2014
By Bozzuto Communities
Park Lane Seaport

It's hard to keep track of Boston's historic sites. But how many of them can you dine in? Union Oyster House near Park Lane Seaport is a designated national historic landmark and claims to be "America's Oldest Restaurant." Located on the Freedom Trail, Union Oyster House offers diners a beautiful 19th century setting in which to enjoy fresh seafood. Diners suggest starting off with the clam chowder and then move on to your main course. Choose from specialties like the broiled seafood platter, the American Bouillabaisse or the fried butterfly shrimp. If you don't have time to dine, stop by and try the Sam Adams unique to Union Oyster House and soak up some American history.

Union Oyster House is open Sundays through Thursdays from 11 a.m. to 9: 30 p.m. and Fridays and Saturdays from 11 a.m. to 10 p.m. The bar stays open until midnight.
