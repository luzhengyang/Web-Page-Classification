Attractions near to Union Oyster House

  • The Paul Revere House

    A part of the Freedom Trail and the home of one of the key figures of American Revolution. Nowadays it houses a museum.

  • Harvard University

    Located in the town of Cambridge, this university is arguably one of the best known universities in the world.

  • Old North Church

    A beautiful church with a lot of history beyond it. The architecture is stunning and the interior is beautifully decorated.

  • Boston Massacre Site

    The site where five Bostonians were killed by the British, often seen as a foreplay to the Revolutionary War. Part of the Freedom Trail.

  • Larz Anderson Auto Museum & Park

    A must-visit for car fans – this is America's oldest automobile collection, both old and new pieces, presented in a delightful way.

  • Dorchester Heights

    Located in the center of Boston, this area is the oldest in the city. Many public events take place here.

  • Frederick Law Olmsted National Historic Site

    One of the most famous architects of the US F.L. Olmstead lived in this house which currently preserves his legacy.

  • New Repertory Theatre

    Thought-provoking and sometimes really shocking, that is the style of the New Rep plays. Theater classes for amateurs provided as well.

  • Prince Hall Mystic Cemetery

    Allegedly the only African Masonic cemetery in the United States. It is surrounded by a small lovely park with a beautiful memorial.

  • New England Confectionery Company Factory

    Also known as NECCO, the candy factory was established in 1901 and since then has been producing plenty of yummy sweets like Necco Waffers.
