Canada’s flagship McDonald’s to open near Dundas Square

By Staff Torstar News Service

Share this Article

Tweet

Related:

  • 5 problems McDonald’s is facing in the U.S.
  • McDonald’s confronts junk food image as sales flag
  • Meat supplier in China sold McDonald’s, KFC expired beef, chicken: Shanghai TV

It’s nearly noon and the coffee isn’t on yet. But the pretty fireplaces built into the walls are, although the fire alarm keeps going off.

The smell of buttery croissants baking in the oven wafts through the air amid the cushioned grey seats and red couches positioned in front of flat-screen TVs at the newest eatery and café to open near Dundas Square.

This is the new land of McDonald’s – a supersized, 9,000 square-foot version with its sidekick McCafe — which is taking aim at coffee giants like Starbucks and Tim Hortons while dominating the fast food market.

“We want it to be a very modern, urban, hip place where you can chill – not that whole fast food, plastic chairs concept,” explained bubbly franchise owner/operator Lindsay Bridge.

The day before Tuesday’s grand opening of Canada’s new flagship McDonald’s location, Bridge can barely sit still even though she couldn’t get her favourite skim milk latte yet from her McCafe.

“I feel like a kid on Christmas morning,” said Bridge, who gave a behind-the-scenes tour of her fifth downtown Toronto franchise of the fast food behemoth ahead of the launch.

And there was certainly lots left on her plate at the last minute to complete the two-storey, 260-seat outlet located in a former sporting goods store and pool hall that comprises Ontario’s largest McDonald’s.

The fryers and grills had yet to have their last trial run by lunchtime, so construction workers had no chance of getting a Big Mac or fries right away. And the espresso machine at the separate McCafe area – where servers will hand-froth the milk and pour it into real coffee cups – was silent.

Video screens and signs were put up for the standard menu items along with a few signs for the seasonal peppermint mocha, which has just replaced the popular pumpkin latte.

There’s no parking at the 24-hour location so the 1,200 boxes of food that came in from the central warehouse Sunday night had to be unloaded on the Yonge St. curb.

Except perhaps for the Zanzibar right across the street, the location was carefully chosen with its proximity to Ryerson University. The majority of the 100 staff are Ryerson and University of Toronto students, and Bridge hopes many more will want to hang out and study in the separate McCafe area. Sixty-seven electrical outlets have been strategically placed on both floors for computers and smart phones.

“There’s no 20-minute seating rule here,” she noted.

The conversation gets interrupted by bells ringing and drills buzzing intermittently, but that didn’t stop the self-professed “McNerd” from talking about the beloved business she grew up in.

Her father was a franchise owner in Burlington when she was growing up, and she has a picture of her dad holding her as a baby at the opening of a McDonald’s in London, Ont.

She started unofficially working at 13 at her father’s outlet and she even met her husband, a former franchise owner, through the company.

“This is our home,” she said quite seriously.

The massive project began in September and involved hundreds of people from real estate and training to design and construction, she said. Besides the typical McDonald’s red and yellow colour scheme, some black, white and grey are infused in the space with modern lamps and lighting.

“It’s tough to build a site like this in the middle of the city,” she noted.

Right up to the official 11 a.m. launch, Bridge said she will be doing kitchen test runs for cooking and tasting. And they will be mopping floors and cleaning tables from the construction dust.

The opening will include a red carpet with velvet ropes where Ronald McDonald will hand out 356 bags of McCafe coffee (they are located at 356 Yonge St.) And for the first two weeks they will include food specials priced at $3.56.

How the new location McSizes up:

The location in Moscow is still considered by the chain to be the biggest in the world, with over 700 seats in 2,000 square feet.

The location in La Porte du Nord, Quebec is considered by company to be the largest in Canada with 12,000 square feet and 210 seats.

The largest McDonald’s ever was the temporary one built for the London Olympics in 2012, seating 1,500 over 3,000 square feet.

The first McDonald’s built outside the U.S. was in Richmond, B.C. in 1967.
