We spent 4 nights at the Fontainebleau, sleeping in a suite in the Tower Tresor at the 32nd floor. If you plan to stay at this Tower ask for a high floor otherwise the view is covered by the nearby building (Sorrento). The view from our room was breathtaking, the ocean on a side and the city on the other, simply marvelous. However, the suite in its self is not that exceptional, not so luminous and the furnishing is so and so. The bath is nice with a Jacuzzi tub and quite big. A stylish element is the Mac console with free internet connection.
The design of the main building (Chateau) is terrific, wonderful modern design with excellent materials, furnishing and details.
The restaurants offer a high quality dining. We tried the Italian Scarpetta, where the selection of wines is excellent (I'm Italian!), even if pricey, and the food tastes real Italian. We tried also La Cote, located between the pool and the ocean, and we simply loved it. The food is ok but what really makes the difference is the environment: there is a DJ that mix wonderful electronic music , some trip-hop, trance. Great! Not the awful hip-hop that you find everywhere! It was so cozy that our dinner lasted almost 5 hours!
Every day we had breakfast at Vida, where the best value is the buffet menu with a good selection of different things, everything really good quality.
The club LIV is another MUST. Great music and great environment.

The only thing I found disappointing is the service. Even if there are many employees they are absolutely untrained. They did many mistakes during our staying (they forget our order at breakfast, the bill was wrong, they were not able to give any indication about the nearby facilities, etc.).

But anyway, the structure is excellent and the quality of the facilities very high.
In general we had a wonderful time and we can't wait to come back again.

A curiosity. One of the day we visited the Fountainebleau (Monday 26th), President Obama was doing fund raising for the Democrats in Miami and he slept in the presidential suit on the 37 floor of our Tower!

I stayed here a year ago and came back to see if it improved. I am a principal of a company that held a company retreat here during this past weekend. F&B got better; they have a breakfast buffet now at the coffee shop. I didn't like the menu but what I had wasn't bad. My service wasn't bad (although I told the waiter I didn't want coffee and he gave it to me anyway and put it on my bill but later took it off), but I heard horror stories from others. Early dinner at the poolside restaurant was excellent and the service in this particular eatery was very good. One point I found on this trip --- in the main building and the Tressor building, the rooms do have closets that you can hang things in. Just the Sorrento building doesn't -- I had stayed there last year and assumed the whole hotel was that way.

Other than that, this place is still a mess with regard to service. Ask the front desk for anything, they say they will call you back and they don't. This happened twice with me during my stay. Ask for a replacement mouse for your in-room computer and it never comes. Ask them to fix the battery that is making the door keys inoperable and they don't fix it so you have to keep calling security to get into your room. Call up Guest Services and ask for a transfer to Cabana Rental and they are clueless where to send you; it turned out that the number was speed-dialed on my phone and I just called it myself after i realized it, but it's nuts that they are clueless. I also don't like that they charge you to use the gym. There is a free gym you can use but it is a poor one lacking essential equipment. To my mind, this hotel is a primo example of how not to run a hotel. They still don't solicit guest comments and presumably would never read them anyway.

One other major point -- they have this nightclub on site called LIV and advertise it as part of the hotel. I was there with my company and we had about two dozen people staying in the hotel. On a Friday night, they only allowed entry to one person in our entire party -- a particularly good looking young lady. It embarrassed a lot of people in our group and I find it very upsetting that there is no priority to hotel guests or that such a selective type of club is advertised as a hotel amenity. Not to mention that guests here then have to go offsite at least a mile away if they want to go out and find out at midnight that they can't get in because they're not good-looking enough. I think this is really inappropriate. Maybe good for the hotel's image but a real problem if you intend to send groups here with corporate sponsorship. Perhaps only Beautiful People are welcome here and they like it that way. Whatever it is, they are selling very well and presumably have no interest in responding to these issues. Personally, this place is just not my taste nor was it meant to be. That aside, it is a hotel that has tons of investment in it with tons of glitz that's definitely worth a visit but still a very average hotel for staying in unless you totally don't care about service and are willing to accept whatever comes your way.

My mother and sister in law and I traveled to Miami for our second annual girls trip in October. We stayed at a different hotel last year but after the experience we had at the Fontainebleau this time we will be coming back here next year! Loved the Bleau bar. It was the perfect place to have a drink before or after dinner. While there, we ate at Gotham Steak, Blade, Vida and La Cote. The food at Gotham was very good and the atmosphere at Blade was cool, there was even a DJ. We never had a problem getting a chair by the pool every day. One of our servers said this was the off-season so could be why it wasn't as busy. The pool was big and the music was relaxing in the morning and more upbeat in the afternoon. I would recommend renting a float for the pool. On Friday night we went to LIV and had so much fun! I loved all the chandeliers everywhere, the decorators did a great job with the renovation.

I stayed at the Fontainebleau in Miami Beach over the Labor Day weekend and the trip was good until the last night that we were there. I accidentally left my camera in the car. It was stolen from my car after I checked into the valet service. Don't get me wrong, I didn't expect them to replace the camera but it was the way they handled the situation. I contacted them after I found that it was not in my car and they took my report and no one ever contacted me concerning the camera. I had at least expected someone from the hotel security to contact me and take a statement. I wouldn't want someone working in my hotel that would steal things from guests cars. I admit that it was my fault for leaving it in the car but at least follow up with you guests to show them that you have empathy for their situation. For the rest of the hotel, it was nice but I think that they wanted to nickle and dime you for everything. Most of the restaurants were expensive and they even wanted to charge ten dollars to use the gym. I should have listened to my wife and stayed at the W or the Betsy. I think they might have better customer service. Overall, I think the Fontainebleau used to be a great landmark but I think there are much better places to stay in Miami Beach.
