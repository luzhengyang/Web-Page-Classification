At this hotel, striking design, contemporary art, music, fashion and technology merge into a vibrant new kind of guest experience. Guests are invited to enter a world of endless possibilities, where they are free to define a perfect day. A total of 1,504 guestrooms offer luxurious comfort and amenities. The 20-acre oceanfront hotel features an expansive poolscape, a pristine Atlantic Ocean beach, state-of-the-art conference and event facilities, one chic nightlife venue, including LIV, and the 40,000-square-foot Lapis spa. At the direction of culinary celebrities from around the globe, it offers 12 fine dining and casual restaurants, bars and lounges.

I do not usually take time to write reviews, however I am compelled to comment on the wonderful experience that Brittani provided for my family and I during my recent business trip this October. I was booked at the Fontainebleau for 8 days in a nice standard room and brought my family along to enjoy Miami while I was at work. Brittani was not only helpful and courteous, she was very engaging with my 6yr old son about Halloween in Miami and his costume, so far as to draw us a map of her local neighborhood with all of the houses that give out the "good candy" for trick or treating! Brittani set him up with fun experiences at the FB Kids club so my wife and I could have a night on the town and she was extremely helpful in upgrading our room. I didn't realize when booking that I may need extra space to unpack a family during my business trip. When I asked about the room size, Brittani walked me through all of the options, helped me to select a comfortable room upgrade to accommodate everyone and checked in on me later to see if I was satisfied. We were blown away when we opened the door to our suite. All of the stress of the day long travel from the west coast melted away immediately when we saw the beautiful view and the spacious, comfortable room. I immediately felt as if I was on vacation and no longer on a business trip. If you are seeking an 5 star accommodation for vacation or business in Miami, come to the Fontainebleau Hotel in Miami Beach. If you want the absolute best service while you are there, ask for Brittani.

I like this place. We were in Florida for a wedding and had spent the first few days at the Ritz in Coconut Grove. This place blows the Ritz away for the same or less money. Yes, it's a bit like Vegas, larger than life, but it's built for busy and the check-in line moved fast, we got great chairs at the crowded pool and though the lobby is bustling with activity, it's not crowded. We feel excited to be part of the history of this hotel and happy to spend our money here. We opted for a room that faces south and we have a little bit of an ocean view and a little bit of a waterway view. Our friend went ocean view for a lot more but were rewarded with a huge wrap-around balcony. Try your luck offering a couple bucks extra to upgrade your room, you may luck out and do better than the advertised rate! Enjoy.

 2 out of 5

Nov 01, 2014 by Stuart W

Disappointed, will not come back again

We stayed here for six nights during Oct half term, two adults and two children (3 and 6 years old). We paid £3500 for two standard rooms, six nights through Virgin holiday (this is another story...

We stayed here for six nights during Oct half term, two adults and two children (3 and 6 years old). We paid £3500 for two standard rooms, six nights through Virgin holiday (this is another story). During the whole period of stay, we felt we did not get what we paid for at this five star rated hotel, and will not recommend any family to stay with this hotel if you are on holiday with young children. There are several issues: 1) on check-in, despite already having paid the rooms fees and taxes through the holiday company, we were advised there are additional compulsory hotel fees per room per day, US$19.99 for usage of the pool, lounge chairs, towels, etc. If you want to use the spa, there is an extra US$50 per person per day. By paying such room rates, we had expected such services should be offered free anyway. 2) Being rated as a five star hotel, the room in general is ok. However, comparing to other five star hotels, this hotel does not provide slippers, toothbrush, comb, complimentary coffee/tea, and kettle. Despite there are four people staying in one room and requested in the initial days during our stay, the housekeeper kept giving us one tiny shampoo, one conditional, ones shower gel everyday. 3) We stayed at the Chateau building. During weekday, the pool areas are quite nice (the two security guys near the only kids pool are friendly and at attentive). However, during the weekend, especially Saturday, the pool party attracts a lot non-hotel resident, the pool area is extremely crowded and noisy. Some of the people seemed offended by the presence of children around the pools. 4) We dined at the Vida restaurant for breakfast and dinner for a few times. Despite being billed 18% service charge, the service is average, some of the waiter and waitress have very bad manner by whispering and staring in front of the guests. The first impression from the restaurant reception staff was poor the woman was chewing gum and looked un-interested, I don’t think I would place her at front of house. The service charge is automatically added but we got the impression they expected gratuity on top of this. 5) On our last day we stayed around the garden and pools. It was a little breezy so due to health and safety every umbrella in the hotel had to be shut. When I asked if we could move some beds under the shade of a palm tree I was told that they couldn’t move the beds. I just moved them my self and ignored all the jobs-worth’s. If you have a car, ($41 day + tips parking), I suggest you venture our to the art deco and marina areas, there are many nice restaurants with good service and lovely food., e.g. Leslie restaurant on ocean drive and Barceloneta. On check-out, the staff at the front desk didn’t bother to ask how was our stay as a courtesy, the housekeeping staff were keen to get us out of the room despite the delay was from their own bell services. Hence, we wrote this review for any future reference.
