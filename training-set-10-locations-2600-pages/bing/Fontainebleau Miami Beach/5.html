8. Re: convenience store near fontainebleau..please?
May 18, 2010, 8:10 AM

OK!

www.tinyurl.com/BEACH-AG

The Map link above covers the Mid-Beach area. The FB is at 44th and Collins. The 41 Street area runs due west and is mostly Kosher but also has Walgreens and CVS pharmacy shops (all purpose stores w/grocery items) plus a few VERY casual restaurants (Roasters Deli, Chicken Kitchen), and the Forge (upscale dining, very pricey). I think the restaurants at the FB (Scarpetta, Hakkasan, Gotham) are probably strong enough to make going to the Forge kind of unnecessary.

Levanna -- if you want to avoid South Beach altogether during Hip Hop weekend, there are some fun spots to the north to discover up in the 70's. Cafe Prima Pasta is a relaxed Italian spot, Sazon for Cuban, Las Vacas Gordas for a fun grill. The North Beach Bandshell usually has a great Latin concert on weekends (live music, dancing under the stars).

Due North is Surfside/Bal Harbour - around 95th - where you'll find a bunch a good choices and a nice street (Harding Avenue) to walk in the evening. Bal Harbour Mall is here (Chanel, Gucci, Neimans) and you might consider a little afternoon window shopping and a happy hour cocktail at La Gouloue and then dinner in Surfside at Cafe Ragazzi, Greek Place or Adriana. Taxi back to the FB or...

The S bus runs along Collins North all the way to Aventura Mall. You can also hop off on 71st, Surfside, Bal Harbour Mall, or Haulover Beach (fun park, marina, golf, tennis and a designated area for nude bathing).

Finally - if you click on any of my Google map links you can also view other areas of Miami, also spas, clubs, and a shopping guide to South Florida...

Have Fun!!

AG
