Get Out and Get Those Big Bass This Fall

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Friday, September 13, 2013
 0  3

With the leaves starting to change and the cooler temperatures just around the corner, its time for you to start hammering those big bass. These big guys and girls are going to start actively feeding again as the local waters start to cool back down. Its a fact, they just dont feed as often or eat as much in the summer when the temps are up and the oxygen levels in the water are down. Another great thing about the fall is the cold fronts that come through. These fronts lower the temperatures and seem to get the big bass in a feeding frenzy. You will find fishing pretty good right after a front passes through.

So where are the big bass in the fall? Well, they are following the bait fish, and these shad and other little guys are migrating up the creeks, so look for the big bass there, as well as on the flats. And the shad that were fortunate enough to make it through the summer have grown in size, so think about using some of your bigger lures in the fall as well. The key is to making your bait mirror the look of the baitfish in the area. Green weeds seem to attract them in the fall, as do boulders and docks where the water is transitioning from shallow to deep.

Scout out the water you are on, find the bait fish, throw something that looks like them, and watch the big bass tighten your lines. I think you will have some great days this fall if you follow these tips. Good Luck and Good Fishing.

Bacon-Wrapped, Marinated Chicken Wings

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Thursday, September 12, 2013
 0  7

 

Okay, so you all know that I have been smoking food at home like a fiend since the spring. And as my frequent visits to a particular nearby wing establishment will stand as a testament that I love wings (and beer), I had an epiphany.smoked wings. But in true Bass Pro Style, I thought how can I make this even bigger and better and tastier??? The first thing that popped in my head was marinade. And the biggest, baddest marinade up in the camping department is the Cajun Injector Buffalo Butter. This heaven-sent concoction is a combination of buffalo wing style sauce and butterseriously??? The only way that this marinade could be any better is if it had bacon flavor in itheywait a second.how about if I marinade the wings, put a zesty rub on them, and then wrap them in bacon???....BAM, its like that !!!!

 

The party that started when these wings hit my taste buds was off the hook !!! Add a little blue cheese and your favorite ice cold craft beer and you will be wishing every day was football Sunday. Heres how I did it and what you need to git r done:

 

Wings I use the Kroger brand, but any unbreaded wing will do

Cajun Injector Buffalo Butter how many jars depends on how many people found out about your little secret and now expect you to feed them

Your favorite rub Again, it depends on your taste, you can go zesty, or maybe a garlic/peppercorn direction, its up to you

Bacon take your pick I try to go with the local store brand because the marinade and rub could make a piece of shoe leather taste delicious

 

Ok, so thaw out your wings and in either a sealed bowl or Ziploc bag marinate the wings. Over night is ideal, but 3 or 4 hours will do ok. Next you want to apply the rub. I found that putting it in a pan and rolling the wings in the rub works well, but I have also applied rub by putting the meat in a plastic sealable bag with the rub and shaking it, and I have also used our Better Breader. Again, its up to you, I just found that the rub stays on better by the rolling method. Next, wrap each wing in a piece of bacon. You may have to use toothpicks to keep the bacon on the wing, break them in half to make it easier. Load up your smoker with some wood chips (Hickory is my favorite) and I like apple juice in the liquid pan. Get the chips smoking, drop your temp to around 225 and put in the wings. Check them at around 45 minutes and see how they are doing. You have to get chicken to 165 degrees to be safe. If you dont have a thermometer you can poke the wing, if the juices are clear and not pink the chicken should be done. When they are done take them out and let them rest for 5 minutes or so, then eat them !!!! Its that easy !! Good luck and I will catch you next time my smoker friends !!!

Smoking it My Way: Making Jerky, Electric Smoker Style

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Monday, May 13, 2013
   

The first day of my electric smoker jerky adventure I woke up. I went down to Kroger to look for a roast. Then I hung out in front of the Bass Pro Store. The second day of my electric smoker adventure I woke up. I went down to the kitchen to prepare the jerky. Then.Ok, it wasnt anything like that old comedy skit, but my first venture into smoking beef jerky in my electric smoker was fun and turned out awesome.

                         

My research lead me to understand that I needed to use a really lean cut of beef, so I chose an Eye of Round roast that came in around 2 pounds. My plan was to make the jerky on Saturday, so Thursday evening I started the process to allow 36 to 48 hours to marinade. About 3 to 4 hours before I was going to start I placed the roast in the freezer, which makes the meat easier to cut in even inch strips. I cut the meat myself with my Mister Twister Electric Fisherman knife, but I would suggest having the butcher cut it for you.

I made my marinade of 1 cup of soy sauce, 1 cup of Worcestershire sauce, cup of Cholula hot sauce, 2 tablespoons of liquid smoke and 6 tablespoons of ground peppercorns. I mixed all of these together in a bowl and placed the cut meat in the mixture. I added just enough water so that the meat was completely submerged, covered the bowl and place in the fridge.

         

Saturday midday I got the meat out and placed it on the smoker racks. I have learned I need to pre-smoke my smoker by heating up the chips until they start smoking, then put the meat in. In order to keep the temps in the smoker low enough for jerky I didnt close the door, which allowed some smoke to escape but not enough to make a difference. I cooked the jerky on low for 90 minutes, until the meat was starting to get dry; it was pliable but didnt break. I removed the racks and brushed the remaining marinade on the jerky and put it back in for 30 minutes. Took it out and left it on the racks for 6 hours then put it in Ziploc bags and in the fridge. The next day when I took it out of the fridge, WOW !! It had a great taste and was even better when I allowed it to get to room temperature later.

Until my next smoking adventure, this is Chef Dave signing off. Bon Appetit !!

How To Get Out Of Spring Cleaning For the Year?

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Thursday, April 18, 2013
   

Spring cleaning what a drag, your wife has found her way into the basement and there is a ton of junk that needs to be cleaned out and thrown away. But honey there is fishing to be done and I want to go to Bass Pro Shops!  Oh no! she says not until you are done with my list! The list which goes a mile long...

Why not simply take inventory of what is cluttering the basement or shed and see what can be done with it? It is best to repurpose an item rather than throwing it away to rust away in a waste land. Dont let those chores hold you down, turn them into something fun.  For instance old hub caps can be converted into flowering beds for impatiens, old enamel ware pots and pans make great flowering baskets as well or an old wabucket. Old small wooden ladders also make great resting spots for clay jars to plant flowers in. Remember that old rake she told you to throw away, flip it upside down and turn it into a wine glass holder. Oh no what are you to do with those old canning jars?  They make great vases for flowers or to place tiny votive candles.

 Bass Pro Shops is proud to support many conservation groups, in which the main mission is to protect the habitats in which these fish and mammals thrive in and to preserve others the experience of fishing and hunting. It is never fun to go out bass fishing only to think you have landed a hawg only to reel in a paint can. So when your wife returns home from a day of shopping with your mother in law she can see all the yard work you have put in you will certainly hear her say Oh honey you can fish whenever you want!

Do You have any Tips for Pre-Spawn Bass Fishing?

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Monday, April 15, 2013
   

Q: Me and my buddy are looking to do a little pre-spawn bass fishing this weekend.  Any tips on what lures we should be throwing?

A: The girls are looking to add some weight before the spawn takes place and they are feeding fast and furious. Your best bet baits are lip-less crank baits, spinnerbaits and jerk-baits. Work the transition areas between the main lake and spawning flats. These fish are cruising so you may need to return to areas that you think they should be mulitiple times before you hook up. Good Luck!!

How can I Call in a Tentative Spring Gobbler?

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Monday, April 15, 2013
   

Q: Do you have any tips for connecting on a Spring Gobbler? I'll often hear birds call back but can never get them to come all the way in.

A: Play Hard to Get: Don't Overcall for Spring Turkeys

If you get a hot gobbling bird that just goes quiet hold tight and wait.  Old birds will sometimes shut-up and sneak in to find his new girlfriend. Keep looking in the direction that you last heard him and give him more time than you think you should. Also don't keep calling. One of the biggest rookie mistakes you can make is over calling. If he is coming your way just wait he will get there. If you continue to call it will give you the chance to hit a bad note and draw attention to your hiding place. Good Luck and Happy Hunting!

You're Never Too Old to Start Smoking

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Friday, April 12, 2013
   

So I am a couple months away from celebrating the completion of my 5th decade on Earth, and I have cooked more meals than I care to count. I have baked, grilled, broiled, steamed, fried and microwaved just about everything that is legally and morally edible, but I had never used a smoker to prepare a meal. I had a ridiculously delicious smoked deer dish in my days as a firefighter prepared by one of the guys at the station house, but for whatever reason never had occasion to buy and/or use a smoker. As luck would have it, Santa Claus, with a little help from my mother-in-law, left a Masterbuilt 30 Electric Smoker under the Christmas tree this year. So now I had no choice; I had to start smoking.

 

I picked the brains of some of my colleagues here that have already experience the joy of smoking, asking questions about what to cook, temperatures, times, food preparationthey are glad I am now smoking and not asking anymore. Do your own research; there are a lot of good books out there and the internet has a ton of information that you can read and then make your own game plan. Besides, if its on the internet, it must be true, right?

So I ventured to my local Krogers and picked out a pork loin, and Eye of Round roast, and two whole fryer chickens. Picked up some apple juice and some prepared mustard along with the meat, (and the most essential items, Canoe Paddlers and Sunset Wheats by Leinenkugel). My Bass Pro Shops list included KC Butt Spice, 3 Little Pigs BBQ Seasoning, Cajun Injector Creole Butter and Western Hickory Smoking chips.

Started on Saturday by creating a brine of 1 cups of kosher salt, cup of brown sugar, 3 tablespoons of garlic pepper spice, 2 teaspoons of paprika and 4 cups of water. Brought this to a boil and then let cool. Removed the chickens from the wrapper, cleaned out the innards, and then placed both in a 30 quart pan (if you only make one or brine them separately, cut your measurements in half; you can also use whatever spice you want to jazz up the brine). Next I poured the cooled brine over the chickens and then filled the pan up with cold water until the chickens were completely submerged (I had to use a bowl to keep them submerged; anything clean with some weight with it will do). Placed the pan in the fridge to be removed in around 6 hours.

Removed the pork loin from the wrapper, didnt brine it, although I would recommend brining it using a similar brine as the chicken. Dried the loin with a paper towel and then covered it in prepared mustard. The mustard allows the rub to stick better and creates a great seal for the moisture of the meat; the mustard taste is cooked out with the heat. I then put the KC Butt Spice all over the loin, wrapped it plastic wrap and put it in the fridge for overnight sealing. I repeated the same process with the Eye of Round, but used the 3 Little Pigs BBQ rub instead of the KC. Create your own rub if you like.

Later in the evening I removed the chicken from the brine, dried completely and injected one with the Creole Butter, and the other with a barbecue sauce and butter injection concoction that I made. Just added some melted butter to a bbq sauce to allow the injection to pass more easily through the eye of the injection needle. 1 to 2 ounces per pound of bird is a good rule of thumb for the injection. Make sure you get the legs and wings, but the majority will go into the breasts and back. Then covered both in mustard and rubbed with the 3 Little Pigs rub.

Followed the instructions with my smoker to season it ahead of time, heating it on medium for a couple hours then adding wood chips to it for the last 45 minutes.

Sunday saw me getting the meats out about 30 minutes before I started, filling the cooler with ice and Leinies, and filling the water pan with apple juice and the chip tray with Hickory chips. The recommended temps for each meat was similar, but the loin takes less time than the roast, which takes less time than the chicken. Using the theory that heat rises, I put the chicken on top and the loin on the bottom. Set the temperature dial to a little short of medium, which gave me a temp of around 225, which was perfect. Cracked a Canoe Paddler and started the waiting game.

After about 35 minutes I started to see smoke and could hear and smell the results. Loin was done in 2 hours, roast in 3 and the chicken took about 4 hours. The loin and roast were around 150 degrees when I removed them and the chickens between 165 and 170. I let each rest for 15 minutes before cutting into them. The loin and roast were both oozing juices as I cut them while the outer skin was a soft jerky like treat. The chickens were incredible, both of them had a reddish skin and a deliciously moist interior. The taste of each bite of these were off the hook !!!

Now I know why they say that smoking is habit forming. But, dont try to give me a patch, or some gimmicky gum to get me to quit. I am hooked on smoking now and I am not planning on quitting anytime soon !!!

Is There Such a Thing as a Free Meal ?

Posted by Cincinnati Ohio on Thursday, April 11, 2013
   

Well the short answer is maybe , it all depends on what your perspective of free is. If you dont mind getting a little exercise and putting in a little book and dirt time in then the answer becomes yes. In todays society we have become dependent on prepackaged grocery food, or running to the local convenience store for whatever our current desire is.

The truth is before all of the modern avenues that are available for us today to obtain food were around most people in this country foraged or grew what they needed to sustain life. I still remember my grandma telling me of how she would make dandelion greens for my grandpa every spring , or how everyone got excited when the wild Paw Paws were ripe and ready to eat.

Natures bounty is endless if youre willing to open your eyes to it. Just yesterday I took a walk down near the levee , this was my first walk  there this spring . I found a nice patch of  Wild Mustard Greens that I would be able to harvest at least three times if desired. I also found several basal rosettes of Bull Thistle that I will go back and harvest a little later in the spring. Last fall we found two bags full of Meadow Mushrooms in this same area that my son Parker and I picked. All of this was located in a quarter mile stretch of land.

Now all that being said you do need to do your homework , or find someone locally who can positively identify the wild edibles for you. If you cannot identify a wild edible 100 percent you should not be putting it in your mouth. Bass Pro Shops carries several survival books that contain information on wild edibles , swing in and check them out. There are also apps available and endless websites on foraging and wild edibles. So take a stroll , keep your eyes open and see what Mother Earth has to offer you this spring.

 

Justin C. Stearns    April 10, 2013
