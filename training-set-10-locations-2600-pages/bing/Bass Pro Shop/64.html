    Bass pro is the best and almost only thing left at cincinnati mills mall. This location is two floors of an outdoors man's paradise!
    The staff is very friendly, and knowledgeable about there products.
    Everything is also pretty organized with the gun area, archery, knives and ammo being in the same area. (They have a pretty good selection of guns, however still no cabelas in that aspect) The hunting clothing being in own area. And then the regular clothes so on and so forth.
    My favorite thing about this store tho is that on the bottom level they actually sell atvs, bass boats, and pontoons! And they have a pretty good selection of them at that! Also at the bottom is a whole section dedicate to fishing with a nice selection of poles and bait.
    Basically all in all they have about anything you would ever want/need! You should definitely stop in and check it out you won't be disappointed!

    Bass Pro Shops opened years ago with the hopes that the Cincinnati Mills mall complex would take off. Unfortunately, the mall has underperformed relative to local area malls and this anchor store finds itself in a bit of limbo. It is one of the best all around outdoor activity stores in the Cincinnati. You can buy anything from boats, to guns, to kayaks, to tents, etc etc. The prices are right and they even have a live fish display. On occasion, professional fisherman will fish in the in-store tank.

    Be sure to check this place out at the day after Thanksgiving sale. Great deals and good values.

    The store is aesthetically pleasing as well. It looks very outdoorsy and fits the profile.

    Enjoy!

    My wife likes Bass Pro Shops because they host free holiday-themed photos.  Bring your kid in for a pic with Santa at Christmas, or with Charlie Brown and Linus in the pumpkin patch for Halloween, that kind of thing.

    I like that, too, but they also have a ton of quality clothing options.  No suits and ties, but plenty of good shirts, pants, socks, jackets, etc.  Some of the items are expensive, but most are reasonably priced, and they often have sales (or maybe they just coordinate them with the holiday pictures, who knows?).

    The sales people here are all very friendly, stopping to admire the kids, ask if they can help, etc.  Though we're still a few years out from buying any of the kids their first rifle, I expect we'll come here when the time comes.

    I'll definitely be back.

    Over the last few years it have probably spent close to ten thousand dollars here. It's a one stop shop for just about everything an outdoorsman would need. I have purchased firearms,ammo,fishing tackle and equipment, tents, backpacking gear,survival gear and just about everything here. The bass pro brand (red head) is very high quality and priced fairly reasonable. Most of the staff here is very knowledgeable about their products and everyone has been very friendly and easy to deal with. The only thing I would like to see is more staffing in the gun department as it can get very busy there during the evenings and weekends. It would also be nice to see a little bigger selection in the backpacking/survival department. Over all it's a great place to shop and the taxidermy and fish tank with waterfall is fun for the whole family. It will be nice to see what the new store will look like after it is built.

    If you're a hunter, fisherman, kayaker, repeller, camper, boater, or just enjoy the outdoors, Bass Pro Shop has something to sell you. I've never seen so many variations of fishing lures, reels, and line all in one place (and that department is only a small corner of the entire department store). This place is really busy on the weekends, so if you have the chance, try to visit during the week when there is less of a crowd.

    This place is a little a bit intimidating. Every where you look there are stuffed woodland creatures, there to decorate a store that sells the weapons that caused their demise. I'm not against hunting at all; it's just a little disconcerting.
