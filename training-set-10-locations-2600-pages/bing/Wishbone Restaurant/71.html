    I was a huge fan of the Lakeview Wishbone when I used to live there and am now delighted to know there is a Wishbone near my new 'hood as well. Two groupons later, one for breakfast and one for dinner, and I can happily report that it's just as good as the other one.

    For breakfast, I got my standard crunchy french toast because I love it- and how could I not with this corn flake covered sweet delight? My only sadness at this time was that they were out of fruit bread. But that's like my favorite thing ever. I would eat loaves of it at a time if I could.

    For dinner, I tried the pulled pork platter with the BoneBQ sauce and brussels sprouts and mac n cheese as my sides. Yep, cleaned my plate. Totally delicious and crave-worthy comfort food. Is it full of the most unique amazing flavor I've ever had? No, but that's not what this restaurant is all about. Come here when you want simple food done right and your belly needs a warm hug, and that's exactly what you'll get.

    I've been coming to Wishbone for years. Over the last couple years I've witnessed the service slowly decline in quality. In the past, service was key from the top down. We would get a warm greeting as we entered and the staff was very inviting.

    Today I brought my daughters here for breakfast and I must say, I was shocked. Our waitress was great (only reason I gave it two stars instead of one) but then we had a minor issue with hair in my daughter's fruit bowl. Not a big deal, we just asked if we could get a new bowl. The waitress informed us that the manager would be bringing it out.

    Now... Hair in food is like the cardinal sin for any restaurant, and I could be posting about that, but I also understand things happen. What I did have a problem with is how the incident as handled by the manager.

    We were told the manager would bring the new bowl of fruit out. After 5 minutes, still no fruit. I want to reiterate, the waitress was great. She asked me if the manager brought the fruit out. After I told her no, a few more minutes passed and a woman who I'm guessing was the manager, brought a bowl of fruit to us. She was originally handing it to me, I then said, "no, this is for her." She just dropped the bowl on the table and walked away. No words no nothing. Almost like she had an attitude.

    Here's where the complaint comes in. If I'm waiting on a "manager" to bring me a bowl of fruit after hair was discovered in the original bowl, I'm kind of expecting an apology, especially since we waited an additional 7-8 minutes for our replacement bowl of fruit. But more so, I certainly don't want to feel guilty because I complained about a hair in my food.

    Okay... Might seem a bit petty. But as someone that frequents restaurants regularly, service is the one element an establishment can control. When the service is poor from the top down, that's a display of how an establishment values their clients. This was clear indication that their management isn't at all concerned about service, which enough reason for me to not return.

    Breakfast/Brunch restaurants in Chicago is a competitive market. It only takes one good or bad review to lose a whole circle of people (potential clients).

    My office went to Wishbone for lunch yesterday to celebrate Juneteenth with "southern reconstructionist food" and we were all very happy.  
    The decoration is kitschy and cute.  My favorite piece was a poster of a cartoon turkey with an American Flag as a cape.

    The service was very good - our waters were never empty and the food came out pretty quickly for a larger group.  Our waitress checked in with us several times after she brought everything out.

    To start, I got a watermelon lemonade to drink and it was seriously good; I had to actively prevent myself from drinking all of it before my meal arrived.

    There are two vegan options on the menu (without making any deletions or substitutions)- I went with the black bean cakes with mango salsa.  This came with vinegary coleslaw, a corn muffin, and two sides, for which I chose sauteed spinach and fruit.  Everything was very good.  The flavors in the black bean cakes were awesome.  They were perhaps just a touch dry, but a couple dashes of Louisiana hot sauce and the mango salsa took care of that wonderfully.  The spinach was lemony and good.  The coleslaw was also nice, crunchy and fresh.  The corn muffin was moist and I ate it as kind of a pseudo-dessert with the fruit when I was done with everything else.  I was VERY full and it felt like a good amount of food for $10.  I've definitely paid more for less food.

    My two coworkers sitting next to me got a catfish po'boy and shrimp gumbo.  Both thought their food was good as well, though one said the gumbo would have been a little too bland had she not added a dozen or so dashes of hot sauce.
    Overall, I would recommend Wishbone to others and I definitely look forward to going back.

History

Established in 1990.

Chef Joel Nickson founded the original Wishbone Restaurant at 1800 W. Grand Ave in July 1990 with 23 seats, nine stools, and a half dozen paintings by his mother, Lia Nickson.
As one of the first chef-owned independent restaurants in the Wicker Park area, Wishbone boasted large portions of amazingly fresh food and quickly became a popular hangout for artists and local businesses of the area.
A second Wishbone was opened in 1992 with 170 seats at 1001 W. Washington--at a time when Chicago's West Loop seemed an uncharted territory. Wishbone was an early participant in the emergence of the neighborhood from a commercial area to one of the city's culinary hot spots.
