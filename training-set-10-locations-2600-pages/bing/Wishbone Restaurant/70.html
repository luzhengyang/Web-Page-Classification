    We got married on June 7, 2014 and used Wishbone Restaurant as the caterer for our big day. Because we had our reception at a Chicago Park District location (South Shore Cultural Center) we had to choose our caterer from a list of approved vendors provided by the city. We met with four or five caterers that fell within our budget. Although several of the caterers we tried seemed to offer the quality we were looking for, we ultimately were swayed by the unique food selections at Wishbone that tend toward Southern cooking.

    The primary catering coordinator who we worked with was Viri Anguiano. Viri was extremely attentive and accommodating throughout the entire planning process. It took us several tastings to put together the perfect menu and Viri welcomed us back every time to make sure that we would be completely happy with our choices. This included tastings for appetizers, dinner, dessert, and even cocktails and wine.

    Viri also responded to all of our questions and concerns very quickly. Even though we did not use a wedding coordinator, we were very comfortable entrusting all the details to Wishbone on the day of the wedding. And everything turned out to be amazing! Our dinner buffet included smoked brisket, bbq chicken, smashed potatoes, among other sides, and everything was great. We are still getting compliments from guests on the food quality! The bartenders from Wishbone were also great and it was never difficult getting a drink. The catering captain for the day of the event was Alejandro. Although we met him later in the process, he too was so accommodating. The whole Wishbone team was just wonderful in every way!

    I would highly recommend using Wishbone to cater your wedding if you are looking for great food and an equally great team to help plan for your special event. Even though we chose from a list of approved vendors, I would recommend working with Wishbone, even outside of those constraints.  

    Thank you, Wishbone, for everything!

    This was my first time at either Wishbone, and it's a pretty well-known place, so I was excited!  We found surprisingly easy parking on Saturday around 11:30 am, and also didn't have to wait for a table.  They had a nice covered patio, but there was a 25 minute wait for that.  Too bad as it might be one of the last few patio-friendly days!

    I started with a glass of fresh squeezed orange juice which was a little cheaper than what it usually costs at a brunch place.  It was so hard to decide what to have, but I finally went for the chicken andouille hash with sunny side up eggs and wheat toast.  I was starving, but my portion definitely seemed more manageable than most breakfast joints.  The hash was pretty good, sausage a little bit spicy and cut in small pieces.  Onions and potatoes all cooked to the right texture.  I had fun mixing my egg yolk around in there.  Eggs could've stood a little more cooking, they had a layer of that clear slippery stuff on the top that I pulled off with my fork and moved to the side.  Not very appealing.  Wheat toast was yer regular sandwich bread, butter came from little packets on the table.

    I didn't taste it, but my friend got the crunchy French toast, which appears to be deep fried.  She said it is her favorite thing, and it did look pretty delicious - her side of bacon was also beautifully crisp as she requested.

    All-in-all it was a really standard brunch.  The flavor of the sausage was the only above-average thing about it.  It was certainly satisfying but very similar to what you can get almost anywhere else.  I'd be happy to come back though, as the Southern menu looks so delicious and promising, I can't help but think some dishes may be good, but others are hopefully great!  Worth a try I'd say, and hopefully they'll exceed expectations!

History

Established in 1990.

Chef Joel Nickson founded the original Wishbone Restaurant at 1800 W. Grand Ave in July 1990 with 23 seats, nine stools, and a half dozen paintings by his mother, Lia Nickson.
As one of the first chef-owned independent restaurants in the Wicker Park area, Wishbone boasted large portions of amazingly fresh food and quickly became a popular hangout for artists and local businesses of the area.
A second Wishbone was opened in 1992 with 170 seats at 1001 W. Washington--at a time when Chicago's West Loop seemed an uncharted territory. Wishbone was an early participant in the emergence of the neighborhood from a commercial area to one of the city's culinary hot spots.
