Wishbone 

1001 W. Washington St.
Chicago, IL 60607
Near West Side
312-850-2663
wishbonechicago.com/index.php?sec...
Hours: Breakfast, Lunch: Monday-Friday; Dinner: Tuesday-Saturday
Saturday & Sunday brunch
The "Big Bone" location of the local minichain specializing in southern comfort food.

Tags: Southern/Soul Food, Music venue, Menu

Our Review

The menu rarely changes, but no one seems to mind. While a few Raters grumble that the food doesn’t justify the hype -- or the crowds -- most seem to agree that Wishbone’s combination of fast, reliable, moderately creative food, brisk service, full patio, and funky decor doesn’t need much tinkering. Lunch and dinner feature upscale southern comfort food such as blackened catfish, chicken-fried steak, collard greens, and buttery corn bread muffins. Wishbone packs them in for weekend breakfast with platters of reasonably priced omelets, eggs Florentine, crab cakes, and the cheesiest grits in town. The “Big Bone,” at Washington and Morgan, gets higher marks for service than the Lakeview location (3310 N. Lincoln, 773-549-2663); there's a third location associated with FitzGerald's (6611 Roosevelt, Berwyn, 708-749-1295).

— Martha Bayne

Posted by Bob McCamant on 06/16/2004 at 10:44 AM

We had been to the Intonation Music Festival which was closeby and were planning to go to Wishbone for dinner anyway....but we also got coupons for free dessert at the festival.....sweet! I'm a vegetarian, which is one reason I wanted to go to Wishbone. I had been there before and knew they had vegetarian choices. My date wasn't a vegetarian, but we both had the Black Bean Cakes with Mango Chutney, my carnivore date had to admit they were fantastic. Wishbone's choices of sides cannot be beat. I had Sauteed Spinach and Home Fries, both of which were excellent....especially the spinach which can be really nasty if overcooked, which this wasn't...it was still bright green and crisp, and he couldn't stop talking about his Sweet Potato side. The dessert.....he had Brownie Pie, which he said was very good, but I had the Strawberry Shortcake and it was more than worth every calorie. A whole biscuit, still warm, cut in half...fresh strawberries lightly sweetened, a little juice and REAL WHIPPED CREAM....unbelievably good. The waiter was really cool too. I hadn't brought the entire coupon and didn't realize Wishbone's name wasn't on what I brought, but he was very nice about it and just asked where we had gotten them and didn't make me feel in the least bit idiotic. Even if we had had to pay for the dessert, the total bill would have been under $30.00 for an excellent meal with good portions. Overall it was a great experience.
