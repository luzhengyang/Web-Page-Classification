        This place is awesomely standard.

        When we got there last Sunday, around 11:30, i was intimidated by…         This place is awesomely standard.

        When we got there last Sunday, around 11:30, i was intimidated by all the people standing around. I figured we would have to wait at least 30-40 minutes to get a table. I quickly put our name in and waited. We only waited about 10 minutes before our buzzer went off and we were seated in the corner table outside. I was impressed that they manage to get people in and out in such a timely manner.

        The service was kind of slow, i waited for a refill of my sweet tea longer than i should have, but they're always walking around with coffee, so my bf's cup was never empty. Food took about 20 minutes to come out, nothing to complain about there.

        The simple fact that they serve sweet tea makes this place note worthy. I never understood (and still dont) why its not served in "the north." I was raised on it in STL (although admittedly, not many places have sweet tea there either), so this place hits home.

        We ordered the crunch french toast to share, and it was AMAZING. I couldn't stop eating it. I also ordered eggs, "homefries," and toast. The eggs were standard, kind of bland and fake tasting though. My bf ordered the shrimp and grits, which he ate every bite of. I don't eat seafood, so i didn't try it. But judging from how happy he was and the fact that he ate it all, im going to recommend it!

        Nothing too fancy or great about this place, but i will be back, if only for the crunchy french toast and sweet tea! But i'll probably go to try out the lunch menu, i cant wait to have cornbread AND sweet tea together again- HEAVEN.

        OH! And this place has grape jelly, unlike so many of the other breakfast places we''ve tried, and i've yelped about. Props Wishbone!         Read more

      History

      Established in 1990.

      Chef Joel Nickson founded the original Wishbone Restaurant at 1800 W. Grand Ave in July 1990 with 23 seats, nine stools, and a half dozen paintings by his mother, Lia Nickson.
      As one of the first chef-owned independent restaurants in the Wicker Park area, Wishbone boasted large portions of amazingly fresh food and quickly became a popular hangout for artists and local businesses of the area.
      A second Wishbone was opened in 1992 with 170 seats at 1001 W. Washington--at a time when Chicago's West Loop seemed an uncharted territory. Wishbone was an early participant in the emergence of the neighborhood from a commercial area to one of the city's culinary hot spots.
