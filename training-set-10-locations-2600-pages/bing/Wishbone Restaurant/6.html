Local Business Spotlight: Wishbone Restaurant in Chicago's West Loop

Posted by SeeChicagoOnline Team on Thursday, October 31st, 2013 at 8:05pm.

Everyone loves the West Loop’s Restaurant Row and what it has to offer in terms of great restaurants, cafés, and delicious eateries. But while Randolph Street continues to get the spotlight—and deservedly so—situated just a block south at 1001 W. Washington is a quaint West Loop mainstay that’s been around since 1990.

Founded by Chef Joel Nickson, Wishbone Restaurant has come a long way since its original 23 seat, 9 stool setting over on Grand Avenue. When opening its expansive 170 seat location at 1001 W. Washington, the West Loop was still very much an industrial corridor with little residential development and even fewer high-quality restaurants. Since then however, the West Loop has transformed into a true culinary destination both tourists and local Chicagoans alike just can’t seem to get enough of.

The food at Wishbone can only be described as a unique blend of Southern style dishes that range from mouthwatering seafood entrées to meaty smothered pork chops that satisfy the appetite of even the hungriest patrons. And while the atmosphere and the new-look West Loop are reason enough to make the trip over to 1001 W. Washington, perhaps the most attractive aspect about the Wishbone restaurant in the West Loop is that all of the food here is made from scratch ingredients. Freshness has been a defining element of Wishbone’s ongoing success over the years and its clear Chef Nickson hasn’t once deviated from his one-of-a-kind recipes that keep bringing loyal customers like us back to one of the true restaurant pioneers of the neighborhood.

Wishbone opens at 7 a.m. Monday-Friday and 8 a.m. on Saturday and Sunday. Closing hours vary depending on the day, as well as the time of day, so for more information or to learn more about what’s made Wishbone so successful over the past 20+ years, visit their website here or follow their Facebook page today!
