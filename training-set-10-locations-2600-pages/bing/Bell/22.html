Clark County Historical Museum

As you embark on your next journey through the Pacific Northwest, be sure to plan some time for a Washington visit â you'll want to head for the coast and check out Vancouver. Once you've made your way into Vancouver â not far west of Washougal and south from Battle Ground â plan for a tour of the Clark County Historical Museum. 

Built in 1909 as a part of the Carnegie Library, the Clark County Historical Museum is a non-profit organization, seeking to preserve and highlight the area. Permanent and rotating exhibits blanket the inside of the museum, giving visitors a real glimpse at the early days of Clark County and Vancouver. The museum is open from February through November. 

Esther Short Park

The oldest public park in Washington, Esther Short Park is definite must-see on your next trip into Vancouver and the WA coast. Established in 1853, Esther Short Park doubles as the town square in downtown Vancouver. Drive in from Washougal or Battle Ground and explore Esther Short Park â check out the bell tower, rose garden, and the historic Slocum House. 

Esther Short Park was named for early Vancouver resident of the same name â dedicated in 1922, the Pioneer Mother statue was sculpted by Avard Fairbanks to commemorate the life and likeness of Esther Short. More recently, Esther Short park has played host to a number of different concerts and performances, and won the Development of Excellence award from the Urban Land Institute of Oregon and Southwest Washington. In 2013 it was voted by the American Planning Association as one of the "ten great public spaces."

Fort Vancouver National Historic Site

Fort Vancouver National Historic Site preserves the Washington Coast area's first military post and premier archaeological site. This historic site encompasses 194 acres of land near Vancouver. In 1824, Fort Vancouver operated as a fur trading outpost by the British Hudson's Bay Company, as well as the center of the community with the first school, hospital and saw mill.

The fort later became a United States Army post. Visit the fort today to see the cultural demonstrations, archaeology digs and National Park Service ranger-led talks. The Lantern tour in the evening is one of the more popular guided tours. Enjoy your time at the site and learn of its rich history.
