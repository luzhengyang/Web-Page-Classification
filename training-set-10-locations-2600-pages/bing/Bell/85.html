Best Mex in the 'Couv'

I routinely eat at this restaurant and I love it. The five stars I bestow upon it can be significantly attributed to its overall value as much as any specific element of the experience.

My favorite pick on the menu is Tacos al Carbon. The beef is tender and borders the realm of spectacular. The Fajitas for two are another favorite for my wife and I.

The restaurant is easy to spot on SE 164th near the corner of Mill Plain by the prominent bell tower atop the three story building that resembles a church. Inside the decor is tasteful and colorful with murals depicting old world mexico. The ambiance is a touch noisy with a family style atmosphere. There is dining upstairs looking out over the main dining area and a cantina on the lower floor with dancing and music.

The wait staff are courteous and diligent in their duties as one would expect at a more expensive restaurant than this one. The food is prepared quickly and is always delivered promptly and piping hot.

This is by no means the best Mexican food I have ever had, but the combination of a great place with great food and great service all at a very reasonable price makes Catedral Tapatia the best mex in The ‘Couv’.

Could be a lot better

As a recent arrival from Texas, I have been searching for a good Mexican restaurant. A fellow Texas transplant, who has lived here for 22 years, said that Catedral Tapatia was probably my best bet so I went there for lunch.

Housed in a former church, Catedral Tapatia is a roomy nicely decorated place. The tables and booths offer plenty of space. Although the restaurant was not crowded (maybe four or five occupied tables) the waiters seemed harried, always rushing around. They seemed incapable of filling my glass without spilling a little tea each time.

The combination platter consisted of an enchilada, tostada, and taco with a side of refried beans and rice. I sadly report that the beans and rice were the best part of the meal. They do figure into my one star rating but not in the way you would expect.

The only complaint I had with the quality of my food was the chicken I chose for the enchilada, tostada, and taco – it was flavorless. It had a good texture, not tough or stringy, but whatever liquid it had been cooked in added no flavor to it.

The big complaint was the presentation, as it killed any chance of an enjoyable lunch. The lunch is presented on a medium size platter with the enchilada in the middle flanked by the beans and rice. The tostada was placed on top so it covered all of the rice and most of the enchilada. The taco was balanced on the side of the platter.

Now, I’ve eaten my share of Mexican food over the years and I automatically expect the “Careful of the hot plate, senior” warning but this plate was throwing off some serious heat. In fact, it was so hot the moisture in the rice, trapped underneath the tostada, steamed the tostada and enchilada. The poor taco got caught in the crossfire and was soaked through from the improperly drained chicken. To sum it up in one sentence, anything on the plate made with a corn tortilla was rendered into mush. Even the enchilada turned into " shredded chicken with corn masa" – not a dish to be savored!

If the restaurant had opted for two plates and presented the beans and rice separately I would have given the meal three stars. If they’d cooked the chicken in something tastier than water and drained it before plating it I would have upped the rating one more star.

I continue my quest for good Mexican food in Clark County…
