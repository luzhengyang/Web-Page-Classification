    “Big Name, Poor Leadership, Inadequate Provisions. ”

    • Comp & Benefits
    • Work/Life Balance
    • Senior Management
    • Career Opportunities
    Current Employee - Truck Team Member in Winnipeg, MB
    Current Employee - Truck Team Member in Winnipeg, MB

    I have been working at 1-800-GOT-JUNK?

    Pros

    Different experience every day. Time flies. Tons of overtime in the warm months. You get payed an hourly wage even if there are no jobs to do. You get profit share on days that your truck brings in a lot of money. First dibs on anything found on the job site. Very flexible hours in the busy months, as a lot of seasonal workers are hired. Get a fair bit of exercise.

    Cons

    Start at minimum wage for doing a very labour intensive job. Professional movers and other junk removal services get payed at least a few dollars more per hour. Does not supply workers with any sort of work gloves, back braces, or safety boots that are required to perform safely. Does not provide workers with basic tools needed to complete certain jobs, but rather relies on tools collected from customers on the job that may or may not be old, unsafe, and unreliable. Does not act accountable for his own company, ignores calls from dissatisfied customers and gets truck team members to deal with them instead of taking the responsibility for his own mistakes in poor job scheduling.

    Advice to ManagementAdvice

    Hire an actual manager and forget trying to juggle this company yourself, along with your others. From all accounts I have heard from your employees, 1-800-GOT-JUNK? in Winnipeg has never done as well, in the past few years, as it has when you had a full time manager. The company is going down hill and your current employees disrespect you as an authority figure. Figure out a way to pay your employees more when they do very labour intensive jobs. Perhaps you could pay out more profit share, go back to putting scrap metal money towards monthly bonuses, or just increase the hourly wage. We spend our days moving heavy appliances out of basements and rummaging through crap (sometimes literally) from random peoples property. Do we deserve the same pay as someone who stands behind a till and moves groceries along a counter? Increase the Minimum scheduled job length! When we have a job booked for 9, 9:30, 10, 10:30 and 11 we CAN NOT realistically complete them all within the promised 2 hour window! If we have to empty our trucks ONCE! in that time, that means AT LEAST 1 hour spent either going to the dump or going back to the warehouse to sort out everything in our truck as metal, donate-ables, or trash. That means we are automatically 1 hour behind on our schedule, and given that we start the morning with a full truck (which is almost ALWAYS the case) that means we are 2 hours behind, and our promised window is shot.
