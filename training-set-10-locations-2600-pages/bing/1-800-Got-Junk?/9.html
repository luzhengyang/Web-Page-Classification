By Ashton Lubman

I joined 1-800-GOT-JUNK? in 2003, having just completed the second year of my undergraduate degree at the University of Toronto. I heard about 1-800-GOT-JUNK? from my best friend, who like me, just returned home from school, and like me desperately needed a summer job.

1-800-GOT-JUNK? was relatively unknown in 2003 and the junk removal and junk disposal industry was still in its infancy. I remember customers being blown away by our service - clean cut college kids who came to your home or office and removed anything and everything from wherever it was. The WOW factor came from the way we carried out the work: with a smile.

We were having fun helping our customers overcome their obstacles and that attitude was infectious for everyone involved. From digging out cinderblocks, to clearing out movie shoots, to doing all-day house clear outs, every minute of every day was different – which made junking such a great summer job.

Being so enamored with the junk brand and the people running it, I made a push to stay on with the business in a different capacity after finishing school. I started by taking on some administration work, which expanded to a junior operations role learning how to manage the logistics that supported a multi-truck operation.

Working in a small business, learning has come from all angles in the years since – how to market and sell your brand, how to support growth through training and development and how to sustain a work culture that rewards exceptional customer service.

Although junk removal as an industry has grown leaps and bounds since I joined in 2003, I believe our brand continues to set the pace based on our commitment through the organization to exceed our customer’s expectations every step of the way. I’m proud to work for an organization that lives in breaths this commitment.

Fifteen years of junk removal in Toronto

1-800-GOT-JUNK? is celebrating its 25th anniversary this year, and it’s made me think about our own big milestone here in Toronto. May 26, 2014 marks the 15th anniversary of 1-800-GOT-JUNK?’s first franchise, here in Greater Toronto. 

I still remember coming home after my first day – I had two jobs on the schedule, did them both myself in my brand new truck, and could NOT have been happier! Since then we have enjoyed continuous growth throughout the GTA. We’ve grown from one truck and no employees when we first started in 1999 to where we are today. Now we have 23 trucks, more than 60 employees, and a management team of eight. 

We’ve done over 180,000 junk pickups in Toronto over the past 15 years. I have many things to be thankful for, and many people to thank for the success of 1-800-GOT-JUNK? here in Greater Toronto. I’d like to thank Brian Scudamore for awarding me the first franchise in the 1-800-G0T-JUNK? franchise system. I’d like to thank friends and family for their support, particularly my brother (who financed the venture!) and my wife and four children who support me every day: you are the joy of my life. 

I’d also like to thank my leadership team, most of whom started as Truck Team Members and have been with me for more than 10 years at this great journey. 

But there are two groups I’d like to thank above all others. First, the men and women who have served our clients as Truck Team Members over the years – you have been, and continue to be, the backbone of this company. As the people responsible for providing outstanding service to each and every one of our clients, your dedication has been the primary driver of our growth. And finally, I’d like to thank the tens of thousands of realtors, property managers, contractors, businesses, and homeowners who have chosen our service over the past fifteen years.  It has been a pleasure working with you. 

My team and I look forward to continuing to serve you for another 15 years and beyond!

1-800-GOT-JUNK?’s Yard Sale Story

Rachel Smith, a resident of the Beaches community in Toronto (and just a few blocks from our office), started Yard Sale for the Cure in 2005 shortly after winning her 9-month battle with breast cancer. The event was created based on the idea that the stuff you don’t need will help find a cure for breast cancer. 

Like most families, many of us at 1-800-GOT-JUNK? Toronto have been touched by breast cancer and the battle that ensues for survival. 1-800-GOT-JUNK? Toronto’s operations manager, Ashton Lubman’s, mother is a breast cancer survivor. 

Given what 1-800-GOT-JUNK? does each and every day, it just made sense to partner with Rachel Smith and in turn our customers, to collect items and host one of the city’s largest Yard Sale for the Cure events.

Our first event took place in May, 2006 in the driveway of a community church. To prepare for that event, we kept the best items we picked up the previous week and sold them to passersby’s for a total of $5,161.  We have participated in the Yard Sale every year since, each year expanding our sale and improving our fundraising efforts.    

Over the past 8 years, we have fundraised over $150,000!
